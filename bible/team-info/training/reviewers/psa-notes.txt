====== Standard Reviewer Notes for Psalms ======

This is a growing list of 'standard notes' that occur frequently in Psalms. Please use these so that there are consistent notes about the same topic throughout the book.

When a standard note includes brackets (see examples below), replace the brackets with the detail that is appropriate for the Psalm you are working on.


**Put this on the first chunk of each psalm.** (The tA team will put in the link to "Translating Poetry" once that page is completed.) \\ 
See: talink Translating Poetry and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]]\\ 

<code>
See: talink Translating Poetry and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]]
</code> 

**A psalm of [composer]** - This can also be written as a sentence. AT: "This is a psalm of [composer] or "This is a psalm that [composer] wrote"

**on stringed instruments** - This can also be written as a sentence. AT: "It is to be accompanied by people playing stringed instruments" or "People should play stringed instruments with this song"

**set to the [ABC] style** - This may refer to a style of music.

==== Standard Notes for First Chunk ====
Use these notes if the heading does not already tell this. \\
These songs belong to more than one category: 9:1-10, 14, 27, 36, 40, 77, 94, and 107, so it may not work well to write one of these notes. \\ 

This psalm is a prayer for help. \\ 
This psalm is a song of praise. \\ 
This psalm is a song of thanksgiving. \\ 
This psalm is a song about God being faithful. \\ 
This psalm is a song about Jerusalem being God's dwelling place.\\
This psalm is a song about God being King. \\ 
This psalm is a song of praise to the king of Israel.\\ 
This psalm is a song for people to sing as they travelled to Jerusalem. \\
This psalm is a song that teaches people. \\ 
This psalm is a song for people to sing at the temple.
