====== Numbers Chapter 29 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What did Yahweh say the people of Israel must do on the first day of the seventh month?

A. On the first day of the seventh month the people of Israel must have a holy assembly to honor Yahweh, do no normal work, blow the trumpets, and offer a burnt offering, grain offering and a male goat as well as the regular burnt offering, grain offering, and drink offerings. [29:1-6]

Q? What did Yahweh say the people of Israel must do on the tenth day of the seventh month?

A. On the tenth day of the seventh month the people of Israel must have a holy assembly to honor Yahweh, deny themselves food and do no work, offer a burnt offering, grain offering and a male goat as well as the sin offering of atonement, the regulat burnt offering, its grain offering, and their drink offering. [29:7-11]

Q? What did Yahweh say the people of Israel must do on the fifteenth day of the seventh month?

A. On the fifteenth day of the seventh month the people of Israel must have a holy assembly to honor Yahweh, do no normal work, keep the festival for him seven days, offer a burnt offering witch would include thirteen young bulls, two rams, and fourteen male lambs without blemish, the grain offerings, one male goat as a sin offering in addition to the regular burnt offering, its grain offering, and the drink offering with it. [29:12-16]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the second day of the assembly?

A. On the second day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer twelve young bulls, two rams, and fourteen male lambs without blemish, a grain offering and the drink offerings as commanded, a male goat as a sin offering in addition to the regular burnt offering, its grain offering and their drink offering. [29:17-19]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the third day of the assembly?

A. On the third day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer eleven bulls, and all the sacrifices of the previous day. [29:20-22]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the fourth day of the assembly?

A. On the fourth day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer ten bulls and all the secrifices of the previous day. [29:23-29]
