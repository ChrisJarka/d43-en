===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **For the yoke of his burden, the beam across his should, the rod of his oppressor, you have shattered **- This is a doublet stating the same thing in three different ways and can be translated as, "<font 14px line-height: 19px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>You will cause us to no longer be slaves of those who captured us; you will lift the heavy burdens from our shoulders. It will be as though you have broken the weapons of those who oppressed us." (UDB)</font> This is also a metaphor where  these words show that the condition of the people have been completely changed from slaves to free. (See: Doublet and Metaphor)
  * **as on the day of Midian **- This is referring to a past battle when the army of the Midian people-group were destroyed.
  * **For every boot treading in the tumult and the garments rolled in blood **- "<font 14px line-height: 19px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>The boots that the enemy soldiers wore and their clothing which has stains of blood on them" (UDB)</font>
  * **fuel for the fire **- The clothing of war is something to be burned up in a big fire.
