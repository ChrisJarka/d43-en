====== Job Chapter 13 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What does Job say about his eye, his ear and what Job knows?

A. Job says that his eye has seen, and his ear has heard what God does to others. [13:1-2]

Q? Who would Job like to talk to instead of his friends and why?

A. Job would rather speak to the Almighty and have the opportunity to reason with God. [13:3]

Q? What does Job say his friends do with truth and wish they did to show their wisdom?

A. Job says that his friends whitewash the truth with lies and he wants them to hold their peace as a sign of wisdom. [13:4-5]

Q? What does Job want his friends to hear as he questions them about their deceitful talk to God and they argue as attorneys for God?

A. Job wants his friends to listen to what he has to say about how they speak unrighteously and deceitfully to God instead of showing God kindness and acting as attorneys for God through their arguing. [13:6-8]

Q? What does Job say about God's approval or reproval of his friends?

A. Job is telling his friends through questions that he does not think God would be pleased with their arguments against him if God judged them, rather he would reprove them when they show him favortism. [13:]

Q? What does Job think about his friends sayings and defenses?

A. Job thinks that his friends statements are proverbs made of ashes and their defenses are made of clay. [13:12]

Q? What does Job say to his friends so that he may speak?

A. Job asks them to be quiet so that he can speak. [13:13]

Q? What does Job say about his own defense to God?

A. Job says that he will defend himself to God until the point where he is killed and has no hope. [13:15]

Q? Why does Job think that he should be acquitted?

A. Job states that he should be acquitted because he does not come before God like a godless man. [13:16]

Q? What does Job say about his innocence?

A. Job has set his defense in order and knows that he is innocent. [13:18]

Q? What does Job say he will do if he is proven wrong?

A. If Job is proven wrong, he would be quiet and die. [13:19]

Q? What two things does Job want God to do for him?

A. Job wants God to withdraw his oppressive hand from Job and to not bring fear through God's terrors. [13:20-21]

Q? What does Job say about direct communication with God?

A. Job would like God to call him so that he can answer, and speak before God and hear God's answer to Job. [13:22]

Q? What does Job say about his transgressions and sins?

A. Job asks God to let Job know his transgression and sins. [13:23]

Q? What makes Job feel like God is treating him like an enemy?

A. Job thinks God is treating him like an enemy because God hides his face from Job. [13:24]
