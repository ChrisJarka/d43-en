====== memorial, memorial offering ======

===== Definition: =====

The term "memorial" refers to an action or object that causes someone or something to be remembered.

  * <font 14px line-height: 19.600000381469727px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>This word is also used as an adjective to describe something that is to remind them of something, as in a "memorial offering," a "memorial portion" of a sacrifice or "memorial stones."</font>
  * <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>In the Old Testament memorial offerings were made so the Israelites would remember what God had done for them.</font>
  * God told the Israelite priests to wear special clothing with memorial stones having the names of the twelve tribes of Israel engraved on them. These were perhaps to remind them of God's faithfulness to them.
  * In the New Testament, God honored a man named Cornelius because of his charitable deeds for the poor. These deeds were said to be a memorial before God.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>This could also be translated as, "lasting reminder."</font>
  * A "memorial stone" could be translated as, a "stone to remind them (of something)."
