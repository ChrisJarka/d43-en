====== 1 Samuel Chapter 08 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? In what ways did the two sons of Samuel, who were judges, not walk in the ways of their father?

A. The two sons of Samuel chased after dishonest gain, taking bribes and perverting justice. [8:1-3]

Q? What was the request which the elders of Israel made of Samuel?

A. The elders of Israel told Samuel that since you are old and your sons do not walk in your ways, now appoint for us a king to judge us like all the nations. [8:4-5]

Q? What did Samuel do when he was displeased by the request from the elders of Israel to give them a king?

A. Samuel was displeased by the request so he prayed to Yahweh. [8:6]

Q? Why did Yahweh tell Samuel to obey the voice of the people in everything they would say to him?

A. Yahweh told Samuel to obey the voice of the people because they were not rejecting him, but they were rejected Yahweh from being king over them. [8:7]

Q? What was the warning which Yahweh told Samuel to give to the people of Israel?

A. Yahweh told Samuel to solemnly warn the people of Israel about the way in which the king would rule over them. [8:8-9]

Q? What was the warning which Samuel gave to the people of Israel about the way in which the king would take their sons from them?

A. Samuel warned the people that the king would reign over them, taking their sons to be horsemen and soldiers, making them plow the ground and reap the harvest, and using others to make weapons and implements of war. [8:10-12]

Q? What was the warning which Samuel gave to the people of Israel about what the king would do with their daughters, their property, and their harvest?

A. Samuel warned the people that the king would take their daughters to be perfumers, cooks, and bakers, take the best of their fields, vineyarsds, and orchards, and take a tenth of their harvest. [8:13-15]

Q? What was the warning which Samuel gave to the people of Israel about what the king would do with their servants, young men, and animals?

A. Samuel warned the people that the king would take their servants, the best of their young men and donkeys, and a tenth of their flocks and make them slaves. [8:16-17]

Q? What did Samuel warn the people of Israel would happen when they would cry out because of the king which they had chosen for themselves?

A. Samuel warned the people that when they would cry out to Yahweh, he would not answer them. [8:18]

Q? How did the people of Israel respond to the warnings from Samuel?

A. The people of Israel insisted that they still wanted a king over them so they could be like the other nations and have a king to judge them and fight their battles. [8:19-20]

Q? What was the response of Yahweh when Samuel repeated the words of the people into his ears?

A. Yahweh still told Samuel to obey the voice of the people and give them a king. [8:21-22]
