====== Litotes ======


===== Description =====

Litotes is a strong statement made by negative phrases to strengthen a positive meaning. Litotes are made to say that the opposite idea is not true. 


===== Template =====

Create notes as follows:

  - List the phrase that includes the ----- from the ULB in bold
  - Identify what the ----- means
  - Add TA Link

  * **phrase** - Meaning. (See: topic)



===== Bible Examples =====

=== Luk:11:04 ===

"Forgive us our sins, for we ourselves forgive everyone who is in debt to us. Do not lead us into temptation."

  * **Do not lead us into temptation**  - This is a figure of speech that means "Lead us away from temptation." (See: [[:en:ta:translate:litotes|Litotes]])

=== Act:10:47 ===

"Can anyone keep water from these people so they should not be baptized, these people who have received the Holy Spirit as well as we?"

  * **they should not be baptized**  - Peter is making a negative statement, called litotes, with the rhetorical question to emphasize that these people deserve to be baptized. (See: [[:en:ta:translate:litotes|Litotes]])

=== Gal:04:12 ===

"I beg you, brothers, become as I am, for I also have become as you are. You did me no wrong,"

  * **You did me no wrong**  - This can also be translated "You treated me very well" or "You treated me as you should have." (See: [[:en:ta:translate:litotes|Litotes]])
