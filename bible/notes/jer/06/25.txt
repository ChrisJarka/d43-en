===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - This is either Jeremiah or Yahweh speaking to the daughter of Zion, who represents all of the people of Jerusalem.

  * **for the swords of the enemy and terror are all around** - The phrase "swords of the enemy" represents the enemy with their swords ready to attack. The idea of terror being all around represents danger which causes people to be frightened. AT: For the enemy is everywhere with their swords and everyone else is terrified" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **the daughter of my people** - Yahweh shows his love for his people by speaking to them as a daughter. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:jer:04:11|4:11]]. AT: "my people, who are like a daughter to me" or "my dear people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]).
  * **fasten on sackcloth and roll about in the dust of a funeral for an only child** - People do these things to show that they are extremely sad. AT: "Show how sad you are by wearing sackcloth and rolling about in the dust, as you would do at the funeral of an  only child" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_symaction]])
  * **for the destroyer will come suddenly upon us** - "because the enemy army will suddenly come to attack us"
