====== Isaiah Chapter 01 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Who was Isaiah?

A. Isaiah was the son of Amoz [1:1].

Q? What was Isaiah's vision about?

A. Isaiah's vision was about Judah and Jerusalem [1:1].

Q? When did Isaiah have his vision?

A. Isaiah had his vision in the days of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz and Hezekiah, kings of Judah [1:1].

Q? Why should the heavens hear and the earth listen?

A. The heavens should hear and the earth listen because Yahweh has spoken [1:2].

Q? What has Yahweh done?

A. He has nourished and brought up children [1:2]

Q? What have Yahweh's children done?

A. They have rebelled against Yahweh [1:2]

Q? How is the nation described?

A. They are described as sinners, people full of iniquity, offspring of evil doers and sons that act corruptly [1:4].

Q? What has the nation done?

A. They have abandoned Yahweh, estranging themselves from and despising the Holy One of Israel [1:4]

Q? What is the condition of your country and cities?

A. Our country is desolate and our cities are burned [1:7]

Q? What has Yahweh left us?

A. He has left us a very small remnant[1:9].

Q? What does not delight Yahweh?

A. He does not delight in the blood of bullocks, lambs and he-goats [1:11].

Q? What does Yahweh hate?

A. He hates your new moons and your appointed feasts [1:14]

Q? Why will Yahweh not hear your prayers?

A. He will not hear because your hands are full of blood [1:15].

Q? What does Yahweh tell us to do?

A. Yahweh tells us to being evil; learn to do good; seek justice, help the oppressed, give justice to the fatherless and defend the widow [1:16-17].

Q? What must you do to eat the good of the land?

A. You must be willing and obedient [1:19].

Q? What happens if you refuse and rebel?

A. The sword will devour you [1:20]

Q? How has the faithful city become a prostitute?

A. The city if filled with murderers. The princes are rebels and companions of thieves. You love bribes. You don't defend the fatherless and the widow's legal plea does not come before them [1:21-23].

Q? What will Yahweh do?

A. He will take vengeance against his adversaries [1:24].

Q. What will make you ashamed and embarrassed?

A. You will be ashamed of the sacred oak trees you desired and embarrassed by the gardens you have chosen [1:29]

Q. What will happen to the strong man and his work?

A. They will both burn together [1:31]
