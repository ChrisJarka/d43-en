===== Description =====

Litotes is a strong statement made by negative phrases to strengthen a positive meaning.  Litotes are made to say that the opposite idea is not true.

**Reason this is a translation issue**: Some languages do not use litotes and would fail to understand that the statement is strengthened. They might event think that it is weakened or even canceled.

===== Examples from the Bible =====
>For you yourselves know, brothers, our coming to you was __not useless__, (1 Thessalonians 2:1 ULB)\\ 
By using litotes, Paul emphasized that his visit with them was very worthwhile.\\ 
\\ 
>All things were made through him. __Without him__ was __not one thing__ made that has been made. (John 1:3 ULB)\\
By using litotes John emphasized that the Son of God created absolutely everything.\\ 

>Now when it became day, there was no small excitement among the soldiers, regarding what had happened to Peter.  (Acts 12:18 ULB)

>And you, Bethlehem, in the land of Judah,
>are not the least among the leaders of Judah,
>for from you will come a ruler
>who will shepherd my people Israel.  (Matthew 2:6)

“You who live in Bethlehem, your town is certainly very important” (UDB) or “you, Bethlehem,…are one of the most important cities.

>Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old he will __not turn away__ from that instruction.(Proverbs 22:6 ULB)\\ 
The proverb uses the litotes __not turn away__ to emphasize that he will continue to obey that instruction. 

==== Translation Strategies ====
If the litotes would be understood correctly, consider using it.
  * If it would not be clear, say emphatically what __is__  true.
===== Examples of Translation Strategies Applied =====

In languages that the litotes would not be clear:\\ 

**For you yourselves know, brothers, our coming to you was __not useless__,** (1 Thessalonians 2:1 ULB)\\ 
For you yourselves know, brothers, our visit to you __did much good__.\\ 
 
**the one who sanctifies them is __not ashamed__ to call them brothers** (Hebrews 2:13 ULB) \\
The one who sanctifies them __is very willing__ to call them siblings \\
 
**Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old __he will not turn away__ from that instruction.** (Proverbs 22:6 ULB) \\ 
Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old __he will continue to follow__ that instruction.\\
