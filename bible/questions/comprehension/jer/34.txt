====== Jeremiah Chapter 34 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? When did Jeremiah receive the word from Yahweh?

A. Jeremiah received Yahweh's word when Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon and all of his army were waging war against Jerusalem and her cities. [34:1]

Q? What did Yahweh say to Jeremiah?

A. Yahweh said, "I am about to give this city over into the hand of the klng of Babylon who will burn it and seize you" [34:2-3]

Q? What did Yahweh tell Zedekiah king of Judah?

A. Yahweh told Zedekiah, "You will not die by the sword, but you will die in peace and they will burn your body." [34:4-5]

Q? What happened when King Zedekiah established an agreement with the people in Jerusalem to proclaim freedom for all the Israelite servants?

A. The people freed all of their servants, but later changed their minds and enslaved their servants once again. [34:8-11]

Q? What was the covenant which Yahweh established with the ancestors of the Isarelites?

A. The covenant was that at the end of every seven years, each man must send away in freedom his brother who had been his servant for six years. [34:12-14]

Q? What did Yahweh tell Jeremiah as a rebuke for the people of Judah?

A. Yahweh said to the people of Judah; "You repented of not keeping my covenant and proclaimed freedom for your neighbor, but then you forced them to become your servants again." [34:15-16]

Q? What did Yahweh say would be the punishment for not keeping his commandments?

A. Yahweh said, "I am about to proclaim freedom to you for the sword, the plague, and the famine." [34:17]

Q? How will the people of Judah experience the wrath of Yahweh?

A. They will be handed over into the hand of their enemies and their bodies will be food for the birds of the skies and the beasts of the earth. [34:20]

Q? What will happen to the city of Jerusalem?

A. Yahweh will bring the army of the king of Babylon back to wage war against Jerusalem and burn it so that there will be no inhabitants. [34:21-22]
