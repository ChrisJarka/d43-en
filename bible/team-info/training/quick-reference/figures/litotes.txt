====== Litotes ======


===== Description =====

Litotes is a strong statement made by negative phrases to strengthen a positive meaning (e.g. "you will not like it", meaning "you will really like it.").


===== Template =====

Template fields: (See: [[:en:bible:team-info:training:quick-reference:template|General Template]])

  - (Optional)  State a clarifying translation
  - (Optional)  State the meaning of the litotes
  - **(Required)**  State the litotes in the opposite format
  - **(Required)**  Add TA Link: (See: Litotes)

===== Bible Examples =====

Field reference numbers are included for clarity purposes only. They are omitted in the actual note.

=== Luk:11:04 ===

"Forgive us our sins, for we ourselves forgive everyone who is in debt to us. Do not lead us into temptation."

  * **Do not lead us into temptation**  - **(4)** "Lead us away from temptation." **(5)** (See: Litotes)

=== Gal:04:12 ===

"I beg you, brothers, become as I am, for I also have become as you are. You did me no wrong,"

  * **You did me no wrong**  - **(4)** "You treated me very well" or "You treated me as you should have." **(5)** (See: Litotes)

=== Act:10:47 ===

"Can anyone keep water from these people so they should not be baptized, these people who have received the Holy Spirit as well as we?"

  * **Can anyone keep water from these people so they should not be baptized**  - **(3)** Peter is making a negative statement in the form of a Rhetorical question to emphasize that these people deserve to be baptized. **(4)** This can be translated as "Let these people be baptized."  **(5)** (See: Litotes, Rhetorical Question)
