====== Jeremiah Chapter 07 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What does the word that came to Jeremiah from Yahweh instruct him to say and do?

A. Jeremiah is told to stand at the gate of Yahweh's house and proclaim this word: "Hear the word of Yahweh, all you of Judah, you who enter these gates to worship Yahweh." [1:1-2]

Q? What promise does Yahweh of Hosts, God of Israel, express to the people if they will make their ways and practices good?

A. Yahweh tells them he will continue to live in that place, so long as they do not entrust themselves to deceitful words. [1:3-4]

Q? What must the people do in order for Yahweh to let them stay in this place, in the land that he gave to their ancestors from ancient times and forever?

A. Yahweh will let them stay if they completely make their ways and practices good; if they completely execute justice between a man and his neighbor; if they do not exploit the one staying in the land, the orphan or the widow and do not pour out innocent blood; and do not walk after other gods. [1:5-7]

Q? In what are they trusting wrongly?

A. They are trusting in deceitful words that do not help them. [1:8]

Q? What is Yahweh's declaration because of the wickedness of his people Israel?

A. What he did in Shiloh, he will also do to this house that is called by his name, the house in which they have trusted, this place that he gave to them and their ancestors. [1:12-14]

Q? What does Yahweh tell Jeremiah not to do?

A. He is told not to pray for this people, nor to lift up a lamenting wall on thier behalf, nor to petition him, for Yahweh will not listen to him. [1:16]

Q? What does Yahweh say will gush out as a result of the people's provocations?

A. His anger and wrath will gush out to this place, on both man and beast, on the tree in the fields and the fruit on the ground. [1:19-20]

Q? What command did Yahweh give the people?

A. Yahweh gave them the command to listen to his voice, and he would be their God and they would be his people. [1:23]

Q? What must they do so that it may go well with them?

A. They must walk in all the ways that he is commanding them. [1:23]

Q? What did the people do in response to these commands?

A. They did not listen or pay attention but lived by their own stuboorn plans of their wicked hearts, so they went backwards, not forwards. [1:24]

Q? After God sent his prophets to them, what did the people do?

A. They did not listen to him; they paid no attention; instead, they hardened their necks and were more wicked than their ancestors. [1:25-26]

Q? What is the prophet to say to them?

A. He is to say to them that this is a nation that does not listen to the voice of Yahweh its God and does not receive discipline; truth is destroyed and cut off from their mouths. [1:28]

Q? What is the prophet instructed to do?

A. He should cut off his hair and shave himself, and throw away his hair. [1:29]

Q? What has Yahweh done to this generation?

A. He has rejected and abandoned this generation in his rage. [1:29]

Q? What is Yahweh's declaration?

A. They have set their detestable things in the house where my name is proclaimed, in order to defile it. [1:30]

Q? Why did they build the shrine of Topheth in the valley of Ben Hinnom?

A. They did this to burn their sons and daughters in fire. [1:31]

Q? What days are coming, according to Yahweh's declaration?

A. Days are coming when it will no longer be called Topheth or Valley of Ben Hinnom, but rather it will be called the Valley of Slaughter. [1:32]

Q? What will become of the corpses of this people?

A. The corpses of this people will be food for the birds of the skies and the beasts of the earth, and there will be no one to frighten them away. [1:33]

Q? What will Yahweh do to the cities of Judah and the streets of Jerusalem?

A. He will put an end to the cities of Judah and the streets of Jerusalem, the sounds of exaltation and jubilation, the sounds of the groom and the bride--since the land will become a desolation. [1:34]
