 ==== Formatting Notes about Figures of Speech - Examples ====

Give us each day our daily bread. Forgive us our sins, for we ourselves forgive everyone who is in debt to us. Do not lead us into temptation.'" (Luke 11:3-4)

  * **Do not lead us into temptation** - This is a figure of speech that means “Lead us away from temptation.” (See: Litotes)

But if I drive out demons by the finger of God, then the kingdom of God has come to you.  Luke 11:20 (page for 11:18-20)

  * **by the finger of God** - The “finger of God” refers to God's power. It can be translated as “by God’s power.” (See: Metonymy)

You see, the grace of God that is able to bring salvation to everyone has appeared and trains us to deny ungodliness and worldly passions and to live sensibly, righteously, and in a godly way in this age  (Titus 2:11-12)

  * **the grace of God ... trains us** - This is a figure of speech that presents the grace of God as a person who trains and disciplines people to live holy lives. It can also be translated as “by God’s grace we learn.” (See: Personification) 

It happened that, while they discussed and questioned together, Jesus himself drew near and went with them. But their eyes were prevented from recognizing him.  (Luke 24:15-16)

  * **Their eyes were prevented from recognizing him** - “Their eyes were kept from recognizing Jesus.” The men's ability to recognize Jesus is spoken of as their eyes' ability to recognize him. It can also be translated as “They were kept from recognizing him” or “Something prevented them so they could not recognize him.” (See: Synecdoche)
