====== Psalms Chapter 116 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What reason does the writer state for why he loves Yahweh?

A. He says he loves Yahweh, "because he hears my voice and my pleas for mercy." [116:1]

Q? What is another phrase the writer uses to describe, "The cords of death?" 

A. He also says, "The snares of Sheol confronted me." [116:3]

Q? After the writer says that Yahweh is merciful, fair and compassionate, who does he say Yahweh protects?

A. "Yahweh protects the naive." [116:6]

Q? After the writer says that his soul may return to rest, what does he say his soul has been freed from?

A. "You have freed my soul from death..." [116:8]

Q? Where does the writer say, "I will serve Yahweh?"

A. He says he will serve Yahweh, "in the land of the living." [116:9]

Q? What does the writer say that suggests that he will believe in Yahweh in extreme situations?

A. The writer will believe, "even when I said, 'I am greatly afflicted.'" [116:10]

Q? What does the writer say that shows he intends to serve Yahweh in a way that all people can see?

A. He says, "I will fulfill my vows to Yahweh in the presence of all his people." [116:14]

Q? What is "Precious in the sight of Yahweh?"

A. "Precious in the sight of Yahweh is the death of his saints." [116:15]

Q? What does the writer say he will offer to Yahweh for taking away his bonds? 

A. He says, "I will offer to you the sacrifice of thanksgiving and will call on the name of Yahweh." [116:17]

Q? When the writer says, "I will fulfill my vows to Yahweh in the presence of all his people," how does he go ahead to define what that means? 

A. He says he will fulfill his vows, "in the courts of Yahweh's house,
in your midst, Jerusalem." [116:19]
