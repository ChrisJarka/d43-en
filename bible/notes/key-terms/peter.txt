====== Peter, Simon, Simon Peter, Cephas ======

===== Facts: =====

These names all refer to Simon Peter who was one of Jesus' twelve apostles. He was an important leader of the early Church.

  * Before Jesus called him to be his disciple, this man's name was Simon.
  * Later, Jesus also named him "Cephas"  which means "stone" or "rock" in the Aramaic language. Cephas is most well-known by the Greek version of this name, "Peter."
  * After Jesus renamed him, this disciple was referred to as "Simon Peter" or "Peter" or "Cephas."
  * God worked through Peter to heal people and to preach the good news about Jesus.
  * Two books in the New Testament are letters that Peter wrote to encourage and teach fellow believers.
