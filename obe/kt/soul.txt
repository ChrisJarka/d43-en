====== soul ======

===== Definition: =====

The soul is the inner, invisible, and eternal part of a person. It <font 14px line-height: 9.80000019073486px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>refers to the non-physical part of a person. and means the same thing as "spirit."</font>    is sometimes used instead of "spirit."

  * Some people think that "soul" and "spirit" are two different concepts, while others think they are two terms that pretty much refer to the same thing.
  * Only humans have souls; there are no references to animals having souls.
  * When a person dies, his soul leaves his body.
  * The word “soul” is also sometimes used to refer to the whole person. For example, "the soul who sins" means "the person who sins" and "my soul is tired" means "I am tired."

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * The term "soul" could also be translated as "inner self" or "inner person."
  * In some contexts, "my soul" could be translated as, "I" or "me."
  * Usually the phrase "the soul" can be translated as "the person" or "he" or "him," depending on the context.
  *

Some languages might only have one word for transalting the term "soul" and "spirit.""

  * In Hebrews 4:12, the figurative phrase, "dividing soul and spirit" could mean, "deeply discerning or exposing the inner person."
