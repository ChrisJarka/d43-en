====== Metonymy ======


===== Description =====

Metonymy is a figure of speech in which a thing or idea is called not by its own name, but by the name of something closely associated with it. A //metonym// is a word or phrase used as a substitute for something it is associated with.

===== Bible Examples =====

=== Gen:06:08 ===

"But Noah found favor in the eyes of Yahweh."

  * **in the eyes of Yahweh**  - This is metonymy. Though Yahweh does not have physical eyes, he sees. It can be translated as "in Yahweh's sight" or "in Yahweh's thoughts".  (See: [[:en:ta:translate:metonymy|Metonymy]])

=== Luk:11:17 ===

"But Jesus knew their thoughts and said to them, "Every kingdom divided against itself is made desolate, and a house divided against itself falls."

  * **a house divided against itself falls**  - The word "house" is used to refer to a family or to the people living in a house. It can be translated as "a family whose members fight each other will be ruined" or "a family whose members fight each other will be stop being a family."  (See: [[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])

=== Act:09:27 ===
But Barnabas took him and brought him to the apostles. And he told them how Saul had seen the Lord on the road and that the Lord had spoken to him, and how at Damascus Saul had boldly preached in the name of Jesus.

  * **Saul had boldly preached in the name of Jesus**  - This is metonymy for Saul preaching or teaching the gospel message of Jesus Christ. It can be translated as "Saul had preached boldly about Jesus" (See: [[:en:ta:translate:metonymy|Metonymy]])

=== Gal:05:02 ===

"Look, I, Paul, say to you that if you become circumcised, Christ will not benefit you in any way."

  * **if you become circumcised**  - Paul is using circumcision as metonymy for Judaism. It can be translated as "if you return to the Jewish religion." (See: [[:en:ta:translate:metonymy|Metonymy]])
