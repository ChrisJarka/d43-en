====== Numbers Chapter 19 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What statute or law did Yahweh command the Israelite people?

A. Yahweh commanded the Israelite people to bring a red heifer without blemish which had never carried a yoke. [19:2]

Q? Where was Eleazar the priest to take the heifer which someone killed in front of him?

A. Eleazar the priest was to take the heifer which someone killed in front of him outside the camp. [19:3]

Q? What did Eleazar have to do with the blood of the heifer?

A. Eleazar had to take some of the blood on his finger and sprinkle it seven times toward the front of the tent of meeting. [19:4]

Q? What did another priest have to do with the heifer?

A. Another priest had to burn the heifer's hide, flesh, blood, and dung in front of Eleazar. [19:5] 

Q?Where did the priest throw cedarwood, hyssop, and scarlet wool?

A. The priest had to take cedarwood, hyssop, and scarlet wool and throw them in the middle of the burning heifer. [19:6]

Q? For how long would the Eleazar stay unclean when he had washed his clothes, bathed in water, and gone back to the camp?

A. Eleazar would remain unclean until the evening when he had washed his clothes, bathed in water, and gone back to the camp. [19:7]

Q? For how long would the priest who had burned the heifer stay unclean when he had washed his clothes, bathed in water, and gone back to the camp?

A. The priest who had burned the heifer would remain unclean until the evening when he had washed his clothes, bathed in water, and gone back to the camp. [19:8]

Q? What was someone who was clean told to do with the ashes from the heifer?

A. Someone who was clean had to gather the heifer's ashes and put them outside the camp in a clean place. [19:9]

Q? What happened to the ashes that were placed outside the camp in a clean place?

A. These ashes were kept for the community of Israel to mix with water for purification from sin since they came from a sin offering. [19:9]

Q? What was permanent law about the one who gathered the heifer's ashes? [19:10]

A. The one who gathered the heifer's ashes washed his clothes and remained unclean until the evening. [19:10]

Q? For how long would anyone who touched the dead body of any man be unclean?

A. Anyone who touched a dead body would be unclean until the seventh day. [19:11]

Q? How was the person who touched a dead body supposed to purify himself?

A. Anyone who touched a dead body was to purify himself on the third and seventh days in order to be clean the seventh day. [19:12]

Q? What happens to anyone who touched a dead body and did not purify himself on the third day?

A. The person who did not purify himself on the third day after touching a dead body would not be clean on the seventh day. [19:12]

Q? Why would the person who touched a dead body, but did not purify himself, remain unclean, and what would happen to that man?

A. The person who touched a dead body and did not purify himself would remain unclean because he had defiled Yahweh's tabernacle. That man would be cut off from Israel and he would remain unclean. [19:13]

Q? What happened to any person who goes into the tent and who is already in the tent when someone dies in the tent?

A. Both those who go into the tent and were already in the tent when someone dies will be unclean for seven days. [19:14]

Q? What happens to containers that are uncovered when someone dies inside a tent?

A. Every open container with no cover becomes unclean when someone dies inside a tent. [19:15]

Q? What does Yahweh tell Moses and Aaron to instruct the Israelites about anyone outside a tent who touches someone who has been killed with a sword, a dead body, a human bone or a grave?

A. The Israelite who touches someone who has been killed with a sword, a dead body, a human bone or a grave will be unclean for seven days. [19:16]

Q? What must be done with the ashes that have been put in a jar with fresh water poured over the ashes to help the individual who has touched a dead body or human bone become acceptable to Yahweh?

A. Someone who is acceptable to Yahwerh must take a stalk of hyssop and dip it into the water.  Then that person must sprinkle some of that water on the tent where someone died, the things in the tent including the people. This water must also be sprinkled on the person who has touched a humban bone, a dead person, or a grave.  [19:17-18]

Q? What must be done on the third and seventh day by the acceptable person to help the person who has touched a human bone, a dead person,or a grave become acceptable to Yahweh once again?

A. The acceptable person is instructed to sprinkle water, on the third and seventh days, on those who are unacceptable to Yahweh because they touched a human bone, a dead person or a grave.[19:19]

Q? What must the person performing these rituals to become accetable to Yahweh again do for themselves to become acceptable on the evening of the seventh day?

A. On the seventh day, the person who is unacceptable must wash their clothes and bathe.  If all these things have been done, on that evening they will become acceptable again. [19:19]

Q? What happens to the person who does not performed these rituals stay and will stay  unacceptable to Yahweh forever because they have not sprinkled the water that removes the guilt of sin and defiled Yahweh's sacred tent?

A. The person who did not follow these rituals will no longer be permitted to live among the Israelite people. [19:20]

Q? What law for the Israelite people will never be changed?

A. The law that says those who have not sprinkled the water which removes gulit of their sins must stop living among the Israelite people. [19:21]

Q? How long does anyone who touches the water which removes guilt for sin remain unacceptable?

A. The person who touches that water remains unacceptable until evening. [19:21]

Q? What happens to anything and any person that someone who has become unacceptable to Yahweh has touched?

A.  Anything and any person that someone who has become unacceptable to me has touched will remain unacceptable to me until that evening. [19:22]
