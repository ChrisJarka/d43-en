====== 1 Samuel Chapter 11 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What was the response of Nahash the Ammonite to the request for a treaty from the men of Jabesh?

A. Nahash the Ammonite said he would make a treaty with them if he could bring disgrace on all Israel by gouging out all their right eyes, [11:1-2]

Q? How did the elders of Jabesh respond to this proposal?

A. The elders of Jabesh asked to be left alone for seven days to see if there was anyone in all the territory of Israel who would save them. [11:3]

Q? Why did Saul wonder what was wrong with the people in his town of Gibeah?

A. Saul wondered what was wrong with the people because he heard them crying after they had heard what might happen to the town of Jabesh. [11:4-5]

Q? How did Saul respond to the threat against Jabesh?

A. Saul became very angry and rallied all the men of Israel to follow him and fight against the enemies of Jabesh. [11:6-8]

Q? What did the messengers tell the men of Jabesh?

A. The messengers told the men of Jabesh that they would be rescued on the next day by the time the sun was hot. [11:9]

Q? What did the men of Jabesh say to deceive Nahash?

A. The men of Jebesh told Nahash that they would surrender to him the next day and he could do to them whatever he wanted. [11:10]

Q? What was the outcome of the battle between the people of Israel and Nahash the Ammonite?

A. The people of Israel attacked and defeated the Ammonites and those who survived scattered. [11:11]

Q? Why did Saul tell the people of Israel that none of his opposition would be put to death?

A. Saul told the people that no one would be put to death because Yahweh has rescued Israel. [11:12-13]

Q? Why did Samuel, Saul and all the people of Israel go to Gilgal?

A. They went to Gilgal to sacrifice peace offerings and to renew the monarchy by making Saul king before Yahweh. [11:14-15]
