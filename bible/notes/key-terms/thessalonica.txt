====== Thessalonica, Thessalonians ======

===== Facts: =====

In New Testament times, Thessalonica <font 14px line-height: 17.8181819915772px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>was the capital city of Macedonia in the ancient Roman empire. The people living in that city were called the "Thessalonians."</font>

  * The city of Thessalonica was an important seaport and was also situated on a major highway that connected Rome to the rest of the Roman empire to the east.
  * Paul, along with Silas and Timothy, visited Thessalonica on his second missionary journey and as a result, a thriving church was established there.
  * Paul wrote two letters to the Christians in Thessalonica. They are called First (1) Thessalonians and Second (2) Thessalonians
