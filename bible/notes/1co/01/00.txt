====== 1 Corinthians 01 General Notes ======


=== Structure and formatting ===

The first paragraph of 1 Corinthians is the standard first century way of opening a letter. First the writer of the letter is named, then to whom the letter is sent and then the standard greetings “grace and peace” and finally the author’s prayer for the people receiving the letter. He thanks God for his grace to them and making them rich in speech and spiritual gifts.

Some translations prefer to set apart quotations of the Old Testament. The ULB and many other English translations indent the lines of 1:19, which are a quotation from the Old Testament.

=== Special concepts in this chapter ===

In this chapter Paul scolds them for their disunity. They are claiming to be following different apostles. Finally, he talks of the foolishness of human wisdom as compared to God’s wisdom. God has chosen to save people through preaching the message about Christ.

=== Important figures of speech in this chapter ===

Idioms [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]]The return of Christ to the earth is mentioned in two very different phrases [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]]:  “the revelation of our Lord Jesus Christ” and “the day of our Lord Jesus Christ.” 

He uses three rhetorical questions [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]] to scold them for their dividing into factions and four rhetorical questions [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]] to say human wisdom is not enough to understand God’s plans.  

=== Other possible translation difficulties in this chapter ===

The idiom [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] a stumbling block. A stumbling block is a rock people stumble over. But here it means the Jews have a problem believing that God would allow his Messiah to be crucified and so refuse to believe.
