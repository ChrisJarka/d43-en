====== face ======

===== Definition: =====

In the Bible, the term "face" can be used in several figurative ways:

  * The phrase "your face" can be used to mean "you." The phrase "my face" can mean "me."
  * To "face each other" means that people or things are positioned in such a way that they are looking at each other or that the front of each of them is toward the other.
  * Being "face to face" means that two people are seeing each other in person, at a close distance.
  * When Jesus "steadfastly set his face to go to Jerusalem," it means that he very firmly decided to go.
  * To "set one's face against" people or a city means to firmly decide: to not protect, to no longer support, or to give up on that city or person.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * The term "to face" could be translated as "to look at each other" or "to look at the face of."
  * The idiom "face-to-face" could be translated as "right in front of" or "in the presence of."
  * Depending on the context, the phrase "before his face" could be translated as:"ahead of him" or "in front of him" or "before him" or "in his presence" or "in front of him."
  * The idiom, "set his face toward" could be translated as "began traveling toward" or "firmly made up his mind to go to."
  * To "set one's face against" a city or people could be translated as, "refuse to accept" or "decide to reject" or "condemn and reject" or "pass judgment on" or "look at with anger."
