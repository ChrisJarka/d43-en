====== Introduction to Jude ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

=== Outline of the Book of Jude ===

  - Introduction (1:1-2).
  - Warning against against false teachers (3-4).
  - Old Testament examples (5-16).
  - Proper Response (17-23).
  - Praises to God (24-25).

=== What is the Book of Jude about? ===

The purpose of this letter is to warn them against being led astray by false teachers.  He references Old Testament events and accounts to give strength to his arguments.  Because of this, Jude may have been writing to a Jewish Christian audience. This letter also shares many ideas with 2 Peter including the warning against false teachers, angels and Sodom and Gomorrah.

=== How should the title of this book be translated? === 

Translators may choose to call this book by its traditional title, “Jude.” Or they may choose a clearer title, such as “The Letter from Jude,” or “The Letter Jude wrote.” Jude is the same name as Judah or Judas.(See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|Translate Names]])

=== Who wrote the Book of Jude? ===

The letter was probably written by Jude. Jude identifies himself as the brother of James, Jesus' half-brother. It is unknown whether this letter was intended for a specific church.  

===== Part 2: Important Religious and Cultural Concepts =====

=== Who were the people Jude spoke against? ===

It is possible that the people Jude spoke against were those who would eventually become known as “Gnostics.”  These people believed that Jesus was completely God but that he was not really a human with a physical body on earth. The reason for this belief was based in an understanding that the physical world was [[:en:obe:kt:evil|evil]]. 

=== What is apostasy? ===

Apostasy is the abandonment of the biblical gospel and orthodox doctrine. It is when a Christian begins to believe in a type of false doctrine, which goes against the essentials of the Christian faith. Jude instructs his readers to defend and fight for these essential doctrines of Scripture.

===== Part 3: Important Translation Issues =====

=== What are the major issues in the text of the Book of Jude? ===

The following are the most significant textual issues in the Book of Jude:\\

  * "who are in doubt; and save some people, snatching them out of the fire; and on some have mercy with fear" (1:22-23). Some manuscripts read, "making a distinction; but others save with fear, pulling them out of the fire"
  * "to the only God our Savior, through Jesus Christ our Lord" (1:25). Some manuscripts say, "To God our Savior, Who alone is wise"

Translators are advised to translate following the ULB.
