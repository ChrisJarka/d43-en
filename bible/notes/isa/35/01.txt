===== translationNotes: =====

  * **The wilderness and the Arabah will be glad; and the desert will rejoice** - These two phrase have basically the same meaning. This speaks of these places as being glad like a person can be glad because they have received water and are blossoming. AT: "It will be like the wilderness and the Arabah are glad and the desert will rejoice" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **and blossom like the rose** - This speaks of the plants in the desert blossoming as if the desert itself were blossoming. AT: "and its plants will blossom like the rose" or "and its plants will blossom" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **it will blossom abundantly** - This speaks of the plants of the desert as the desert itself. AT: "its plants will blossom abundantly" or "it will be full of flowers" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **and rejoice with joy and singing** - This speaks of the desert as if it were happy and singing like a person. AT: "it will be as though everything is rejoicing and singing!" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **the glory of Lebanon will be given to it** - This can be stated in active form. This speaks of Yahweh making the desert looks as glorious as Lebanon as if he where giving the desert Lebanon's glory. AT: "Yahweh will give it the glory of Lebanon" or "Yahweh will make it as glorious as Lebanon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the splendor of Carmel and Sharon** - The missing words may be added. Also, this speaks of Yahweh making the desert look beautiful as Carmel and Sharon as if her were given the desert their splendor. AT: "the splendor of Carmel and Sharon will be given to it" or "Yahweh will make it as splendid as Carmel and Sharon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the glory of Yahweh, the splendor of our God** - These two phrases mean basically the same thing and emphasize Yahweh's appearance. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]])
