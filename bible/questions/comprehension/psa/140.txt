====== Psalms Chapter 140 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What are some reasons that David wants resued from the wicked?

A. David wants rescued from the wicked to preserve him from violent men. [140:1]

Q? What does David say about the wicked?

A. The wicked plan evil in their hearts, stir up conflict every day, their tongues wound like serpents and vipers' poison is on their lips. [140:2-3]

Q? How does David want Yahweh to protect him?

A. David wants Yahweh to keep the hands of the wicked away from David, preserve him from violent men who plan to knock him over, and protect him from the trap, net and snare that the proud have set against David. [140:4-5]

Q? What does David say to Yahweh?

A. David said, "You are my God; listen to my cries for mercy." [140:6]

Q? What does Yahweh do for David?

A. Yaweh is strong to save; Yahweh shields David's head on the day of battle, and Yahweh does not let the wicked peoples desires or plots succeed. [140:7-8]

Q? What does David want to happen to those who surround him?

A. David wants those who surround him to raise their heads; let the mischief of theri own lips overwhelm them, let burning coals fall on them; throw them into the fire, into bottowmless pits, never more to rise. [140:9-10]

Q? What does David want to happen to those who say evil things about others?

A. David wants anyone who says evil things about others to not be secure on the earth and to be hunted down by evil and the violent man to overthrow him. [140:11]

Q? Who will maintain the cause of the afflicted that he will exercise justice for the needy?

A. Yahweh will maintain the cause of the afflicted and Yahweh will exercise justice for the needy. [140:12]

Q? To whom do the righteous people give thanks and to whose name do the upright give thanks and live in his presence?

A. The righteouse people will give thanks to Yahweh's name and the upright will live in his presence as they give thanks. [140:13]
