===== Translation Notes: =====
Isaiah begins a story describing Israel as a vineyard that God planted. (See: [[en:ta:translate:parable]])
  * **Let me sing for my well beloved** - "Let me sing for the one I love dearly." Here "me" refers to Isaiah. And the word "beloved" refers to God.
  * **on a very fertile hill**  - "on a hill with good soil."
  * **He spaded it and removed the stones**  - The word "spaded" refers to using a tool to dig into the dirt to prepare it for planting. The stones were used to build a wall around the vineyard to protect it from animals. This can be translated "He dug it up and took out the stones."​
  * **choicest vine**  - "best vine" or "strongest vine."
  * **He built a tower in the middle of it**  – “He made a tall building in the middle of the vineyard to watch over it”
  * **built a winepress**  - A "winepress" refers to a place where the grapes were stepped on and crushed and their juice was collected. This can be translated as " dug a pit to squeeze out the grape juice"
  * **wild grapes** - "bitter grapes" or "bad tasting grapes"
