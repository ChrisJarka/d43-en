====== Introduction to 2 Peter ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

=== Outline of the Book of 2 Peter ===

  - Introduction (1:1-2)
  - Reminder to live good lives because God has enabled us to (1:3-21)
  - Warning against false teachers (2:1-22)
  - Encouragement to prepare for the second coming of Jesus (3:1-17)

=== What is the Book of 2 Peter about? ===

Peter wrote this letter in order to encourage believers to live good lives and to warn them not to follow false teachers.  He told them to ignore people who were saying that Jesus was taking too long to return.  Instead, he pointed out that God is not slow but wants everyone to be saved.

=== How should the title of this book be translated? === 

Translators may choose to call this book by its traditional title, “2 Peter” or "Second Peter." Or they may choose a clearer title, such as “The Second Letter from Peter,” or “The Second Letter Peter wrote.” (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|Translate Names]])

=== Who wrote the Book of 2 Peter? ===

The letter of 2 Peter states that it was written by the Apostle Peter. Peter probably wrote the letter near the end of his life. He was probably in prison in Rome and facing imminent death. Peter calls this letter his second letter, so we can date it after 1 Peter.  He addressed the letter to all Christians. 

===== Part 2: Important Religious and Cultural Concepts =====

=== Why were false teachers such a problem in the early church? ===

False teachers have always been a problem for the church as the devil seeks to destroy the church of Christ. It was an issue in the church church because of two reasons. First, there was not yet a completed Bible. It would have been a lot easier for a false teacher to lead people astray if they did not have the Scriptures in writing to defend the biblical teachings. Second, there was very little knowledge of christian teachings in the world. This lack of knowledge would have been an advantage for the false teachers. These can be important lessons for the church today.

=== What does it mean that God inspired Scripture? ===

The doctrine of Scripture is a very important one. The teaching of this books helps the reader to understand that while each writer of Scripture has their own distinct way of writing, God is the true author of Scripture. This is why it is also referred to as God's word. This doctrine has many implications. For example, the Bible is trustworthy and does not lie or mislead people. It is also free from error.

===== Part 3: Important Translation Issues =====

=== Why are English translation of 2 Peter so different? ===

2 Peter is one of the most difficult books to translate. There are several contributing factors to this. The most significant is that it originally contains many long sentences in Greek. This make the relationship between clauses somewhat uncertain and difficult to translate smoothly.
