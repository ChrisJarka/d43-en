===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **make known to you everything concerning me **- "tell you everything that has been happening to me" (See: UDB)
  * **me **- This pronoun is referring to the apostle Paul.
  * **fellow-slave ** <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>-</font>    <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>"fellow servant".</font>    <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>This is the same Greek word that is translated</font>    <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>"fellow servant"</font>    <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>in Colossians 1:7.</font>
  * **I **- This pronoun is referring to the apostle Paul.
  * **him **- "Tychicus"
  * **you **- This pronoun is referring to the believes in Colossae.
  * **us **- This pronoun is referring to Paul and those working with him including Timothy.
  * **he **- This pronoun is referring to Tychicus.
  * **Onesimus **- Onesimus was a slave of Philemon in Colossae. He stole money from Philemon and ran away to Rome where he became a Christian through the ministry of Paul. Now <font 14px line-height: 19.6000003814697px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>Tychicus</font>    and Onesimus are the ones bringing Paul's letter to Colossae.
  * **your **- This pronoun is referring to the believers in Colossae.
  * **brother **- Here the word "brother" is talking about Onesimus now being a fellow Christian, not a blood relative.
  * **They **- This pronoun refers to <font 14px line-height: 19.6000003814697px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>Tychicus and Onesimus.</font>
  * **here **- This is an adverb referring to Rome where Paul was now in prison.
