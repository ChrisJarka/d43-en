===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Everyone says ... everyone speaks** - The two occurrences of "everyone" are exaggerations, used to emphasize that this is true of very many people. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **everyone speaks with flattering lips and a double heart** - Here "lips" is a metonym for what people say, and "a double heart" is a metaphor for deception. AT: "everyone speaks with flattering words and deception" or "everyone praises people falsely and tells lies" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **cut off all flattering lips** - Here "flattering lips" is a metonym for people who flatter others. Possible meanings are 1) "cut off" is a metaphor for killing. AT: "kill all who flatter others" or 2) "cut off" is a metaphor for stopping something. AT: "stop all those who flatter others" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **every tongue declaring great things** - Here "every tongue" represents every person who declares great things. AT: "every person who boasts" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **With our tongues will we prevail** - Here the word "tongues" represents what people say. AT: "We will prevail because of what we say" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **will we prevail** - "we will succeed" or "we will win" or "we will be victorious"
  * **When our lips speak** - Here the "lips" represents the people who speak. AT: "When we speak" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **who can be master over us?** - This rhetorical question is asked to emphasize that they believed no one could rule over them. It can be translated as a statement. AT: "no one can rule over us!" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
