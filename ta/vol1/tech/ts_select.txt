===== Select Source and Target Languages =====

//This page answers the question:// //How do I select source and target languages?//

//In order to understand this topic, it would be good to read://
  * //[[en:ta:vol1:tech:ts_install|Install translationStudio on an Android Device]]//

==== VERSION 3 ====

You can select and change your source language and target language whenever you use translationStudio.

**Note:** The first time you use the application, translationStudio opens at the **Translation Projects** screen so you can select the target language. 

Complete these steps to select or change the target language, which is the language you are translating TO:

If you are not on the 'Your Translation Projects' screen:
  - Tap on 3 dots located at bottom left of the screen.
  - Choose **My Translations** from the menu.
The App will open the 'Your Translation Projects' screen.

On the 'Your Translation Projects' screen:
  - Tap the blue circle with a + sign
  - Swipe up (place a finger on the white area and slide up) to find the target language or tap the look-up icon located at the top right of the screen. The look-up icon is a circle with a line that looks like a magnifying glass.
  - The search field opens and the keyboard appears. Type the first few letters of your language to show that portion of the language list.
  - Tap on your language. The keyboard should disappear.
  - Choose a project from the 'Bible: NT', 'Bible: OT' or 'Open Bible Stories' to start translating.
    - In the Bible: NT or Bible: OT projects you will need to then choose a book for the project.
    - An Open Bible Stories project will open to the first paragraph of The Creation.
  - The new translation project opens.

Complete these steps to select or change the source language, which is the language you are translating FROM:
In the translation project:
  - A new translation project will prompt you to 'Chose source translation(s) to begin reading and translating'. 
    - Tap the square icon with the + and - sign directly under the prompt.
  - You may also tap the square icon with the + and - sign found at the top right of the source text window.
  - Tap the empty square box next to the desired source language.
  - Tap on "Confirm" at the bottom of the window to save the selection. Or, tap on "Cancel" to close and not save the selected language. There MUST be a source language chosen.

==== VERSION 2 ====

You can select and change your source language and target language whenever you use translationStudio.

**Note:** The first time you use the application, translationStudio opens at the **Project Settings** screen so you can select your languages. To get to this screen any other time, tap **Project Settings** in the green bar at the top of the screen.

Complete these steps to select or change the target language, which is the language you are translating TO:
  - Tap **Project ****Settings**.
  - The gray bar under **Target Language**  shows the current target language. Tap the gray bar to open the list of available target languages.
  - Tap above the list to open the keyboard.
  - Type the first few letters of your language to navigate to that portion of the list.
  - Tap on your language. If you wish to hide the keyboard, tap the triangle in the black bar below the list.
  - When you have selected your language, tap **OK **  in the upper right corner.
  - If you are satisfied with your target language, tap **OK **  again in the upper right corner. Otherwise repeat these steps to select the correct language.

Complete these steps to select or change the source language, which is the language you are translating FROM:
  - Tap **Project ****Settings**.
  - The gray bar under **Source Language**  shows the current source language (English is the default language). Tap the gray bar to open the list of available source languages.
  - Tap above the list to open the keyboard.
  - Type the first few letters of your language to navigate to that portion of the list.
  - Tap on the desired source language. To hide the keyboard, tap the triangle in the black bar below the list.
  - When you have selected your language, tap **OK **  in the upper right corner.
  - If you are satisfied with your source language, tap **OK **  again in the upper right corner. Otherwise repeat these steps to select the correct language.
