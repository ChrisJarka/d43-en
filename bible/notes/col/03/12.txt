===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Put on therefore, as God’s chosen ones–holy and beloved**  - This could be translated “As God's chosen people who are holy and dearly loved, put on”. (See: Active or Passive)
  * **put on**  – The Greek words convey the idea of putting on the listed character traits in much the same way as a person puts on clothing.
  * **a heart of compassion, kindness, lowliness, meekness, and patience **- "a heart that is compassionate, kind, humble, gentle and patient".
  * **heart of compassion**  - literally “bowels of mercy”. This phrase could be translated as “heart of empathy” or “heart of consideration”.
  * **kindness **– The Greek indicates moral excellence. Could be translated as “goodness” or “gentleness”.
  * **lowliness**  – The Greek literally means “humiliation of mind”. This might be better translated as “humbleness of mind” or “lowliness of mind” or “modesty”.
  * **meekness**  – This could also be translated as “gentleness”.
  * **patience**  – The Greek has the idea of fortitude or longsuffering.
  * **Bear with one another **– This phrase has the idea to put up with or endure each another. The idea is to work together in harmony and love.
  * **complaint against **- This phrase could be translated as “quarrel with”.
  * **have love **– This can also be translated as “put on”. See note above regarding the pharse "put on".
  * **is the bond of perfection **– This could be translated as “binds us perfectly together” or “binds us together in perfect harmony”.
