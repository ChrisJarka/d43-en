====== Deuteronomy Chapter 14 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What does Moses instruct the people of Yahweh their God not do because they are Yahweh's chosen people for his own possession, more than al peoples that ar on the surface of the earth?

A. The people of Yahweh are not to cut themselves, nor share any part of their face for the dead. [14:1-2]

Q? What are the people of Israel allowed to eat, as they are not to eat any disgusting thing?

A. The people of Israel may eat: the ox, the sheep, and the goat, the deer, the gazelle, the roebuck, the wild goat, and ibex, and the antelope, and the mountain sheep. [14:3-5]

Q? What type of animals are the Israelites allowed to eat?

A. Israel may eat any animal that parts the hoof, that is, that has the hoof divided in two, and that also chews the cud. [14:6]

Q? What animals must Israel not eat eventhough they chew cud or have the hoof divided in two?

A. The camel, the rabbit, and the rock badger are not to be eaten because they chew the cud but do not part the hoof, which makes them unclean for the Israelites. [14:7]

Q? Why is the pig not to be eaten or even have it's carcass touched?

A. The pig is unclean for the Israelites because he parts the hoof but does not chew the cud. The Israelites are not to even touch the pig's carcass. [14:8]

Q? What are the Israelites allowed to eat and not eat from the waters?

A. Whatever has fins and scales may be eaten, but whatever has no fins and scales must not be eaten because it is unclean for the Israelites. [14:9-10]

Q? Which birds can be eaten?

A. All clean birds may be eaten. [14:11]

Q? Which birds are unclean and should not be eaten?

A. The eagle, the vulture, the osprey, the red kite and black kite, no falcon of any kind, and the ostrich, and the night hawk, the sea gull, no kind of hawk, the little owl, the great owl, the white owl, the pelican, the carrion vulture, the cormorant, the stork, any kind of heron, the hoopoe and the bat, [14:12-18]

Q? Are the Israelites allowed to eat winged swarming things?

A. All winged, swarming things are unclean for the Israelites and must not be eaten. [14:19]

Q? Can the Israelites eat clean flying things?

A. The Israelites can eat all clean flying things. [14:20]

Q? Who may eat anything that dies of itself, eventhough Israelites may not eat these animals?

A. The foreigner who is within Israel's gates, may eat the animals that die of itself. [14:21]

Q? Why should Israelites not boil a young goat in its mother's milk?

A. Because Israelites are a holy people to Yahweh their God, they must not boil a young goat in its mother's milk. [14:21]

Q? What are the Israelites to do with all the yield of their seed?

A. Israelites must surely tithe all the yield of their seed. [14:22]

Q? What must the Israelites eat so that they learn to always honor Yahweh their God.

A. Israel must eat the tithe of their grain, of their new wine, and of their oil, and the firstborn of their herd and their flock in the place that he will choose as his sanctuary, so that they learn to always honor Yahweh their God. [14:23]

Q? What are the Israelites allowed to do with the tithe if the place that Yahweh their God will choose as his sanctuary is too far away?

A. When Yahweh God blesses them they can convert the offering into money, tie up the money in their hand, and go to the place that Yahweh has choosen if the journey is too long for them so that they can not carry the tithe. [14:24-25]

Q? Where will the Israelites spend the money for whatever their soul desires and eat before Yahweh their Lord as they rejoice with their household?

A. The place that Yahweh chooses is where the Israelites can spend the money for whatever their soul desires. [14:25-26]

Q? What are the things that Moses lists as items that their soul desires?

A. Moses mentions oxen, sheep, wine, or strong drink are items specified with whatever their soul desires. [14:26]

Q? Who has no inheritance with the Israelites and must not be forsaken?

A. The Levite who is within their gates has no inheritance and must not be forsaken. [14:27]

Q? What are the Israelites instructed to do at the end of every three years?

A. At the end of every three years the Israelites are to present all the tithe of their produce in the same year, and they are to store it up within their gates; and the Levite, because he has no portion nor inheritance among them, and the foreigner, and the fatherless, and the widow who are within their gates will come and eat and be satisfied. [14:28-29]

Q? Why are the Israelites to present their tithe of their produce in the same year at the end every three years?

A. The Israelites are to present the tithe of their produce in the same year at the end of every three years so that Yahweh their God may blass them in the work of their hands. [14:28-29]
