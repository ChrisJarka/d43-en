====== Deuteronomy Chapter 09 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Where are the people of Israel going and what will they do there?

A. Israel will cross over the Jordan, take over nations and cities greater than themselves, including the sons of Anak who are great and tall. [9:1-2]

Q? What will happen before Israel crosses Jordan?

A. Yahweh will go before them and will destroy the nations, cities, and the sons of Anak like a devouring fire. [9:3]

Q? Why did Moses tell the people not to think it was their rightousness that brought them in the land?

A. He told Israel, Yahweh thrust out the nations because of wickedness not because of their rightousness. [9:4]

Q? Why is God driving the nations out before Israel crosses the Jordan?

A. He is keeping his word that he swore to Israel's ancestors, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. [9:5]

Q? Why was Yahweh angry wiith the people of Israel?

A. He was angry enough to destroy them because they rebelled against him from the day they left Egypt, at Horeb and until they came to the land. [9:7-8]

Q? What did Moses tell the people had happened to him on the mountain?

A. He told them when he went to the mountain, he stayed for forty days and nights, did not eat or drink, then Yahweh gave him two tablets of stone. On them was written, with Yahweh's finger, all the words that he had told them on the day of the assembly. [9:9-10]

Q? Why did Yahweh tell Moses to quickly go down from the mountain?

A. He went down because Yahweh told him the people had corrupted themselves by turning away from the path that Yahweh commanded and made themselves an idol. [9:12]

Q? Why did Yahwey tell Moses he would make of him a mightier nation than they were?

A. Yahweh said he had seen the stubborn people, he might destroy them, and blot out their name from under heaven. [9:13-14]

Q? What did Moses see when he came down from the mountain with the tablets in his hands?

A. He saw that the people had sinned against Yahweh by making themselves an idol. [9:15-16]

Q? What did Moses do after he saw that the people had done evil in the sight of Yahweh?

A. He threw the tablets and broke them before their eyes, he layed facedown before Yahweh for forty days and nights, and did not eat or drink. [9:17-18]

Q? Why did Moses pray for the people and Aaron?

A. He prayed because Yahweh was angry and displeased with the people and Aaron, enough to distroy them. [9:19-20]

Q? What did Moses do to the calf that the people had made?

A. Moses burned it, beat it, and ground it very small and threw its dust into the stream that came down from the mountain. [9:21]

Q? How did the people provoke Yahweh to wrath?

A. They rebelled against the commandment of Yahweh and they did not believe or listen to his voice. [9:22-24]

Q? What was Moses' prayer as he layed facedown for forty days and nights?

A. He prayed that Yahweh's people and inheritance would not be destroyed. [9:25-26]

Q? What is Moses pleading to Yahweh regarding the promise God made to the people's ancesters?

A. He is pleading that God not look at the sin of the people, so that Egypt would not say that Yahweh brought them into the wilderness to kill them, but that he brought them to the promised land with his great strength and power. [9:27-29]
