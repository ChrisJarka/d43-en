====== Introduction to 2 Corinthians ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

=== Outline of the Book of 2 Corinthians ===

  - Thanksgiving for the Corinthians, and defense of his upcoming travel plans (1:1-11)
  - Paul explains his conduct (1:12-2:13)
  - Parenthetical defense of Paul's apostolic ministry
    - Superiority of the apostolic ministry; suffering and glory; function; transparency and joy;  eternal and temporal joys (4:16-5:10)
    - The love of Christ pushes him; he asks them to open their hearts (6:1-7:4)
  - Reconciliation with the Corinthians (7:5-16)
    - News gives him comfort when he is  Macedonia
    - The “severe letter”; help comes to Paul from Titus
  - The collection for the Jerusalem church (Chapters 8-9)
  - Paul's defense of his apostolic authority (10:1-13:10)
  - Final greetings and challenge (13:11-13)


=== What is the Book of 2 Corinthians about? ===

2 Corinthians is a book about conflict. Paul heard many bad reports about the churches actions. Some of their struggles included theological or doctrinal issues but most of them were about the Corinthians living [[:en:obe:kt:evil|evil]] or [[:en:obe:kt:sin|sinful]] lives. 1 Corinthians is Paul's response and admonition towards righteous living to the Christians in Corinth. 2 Corinthians is a letter encouraging the church after they had listened to much of Paul's earlier instructions.

=== How should the title of this book be translated? === 

Translators may choose to call this book by its traditional title, "2 Corinthians," "Second Corinthians" or "Paul's second Letter to the Church in Corinth." They may also choose a title that may be clearer. The letter is not actually the second letter to the church in Corinth because there 2 earlier letters, which no longer exist. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|Translate Names]])

=== Who wrote the Book of 2 Corinthians? ===

Paul of Tarsus was the author. He probably wrote from Ephesus after he lived for some time in Corinth and planted the church there. Paul was also known as “Saul” when he was a Pharisee and a persecutor of the church before his conversion to faith in Christ. He was a scholar in the Jewish law, but he became a Christian and an apostle of Jesus Christ.

===== Part 2: Important Religious and Cultural Concepts =====
=== What was the city of Corinth like? ===

Corinth was a major city located in modern-day Greece. Because of its location on the Mediterranean Sea, many people bought and sold goods there. This caused the city to have people from many different cultures. It was known for its immorality. Its religious practices revolved around Aphrodite, the goddess of love where they had sexual intercourse with temple prostitutes.

=== What is meat sacrificed to idols? ===

One of the controversies in the early church involved whether christian were allowed to eat meat that was used in pagan rituals and had been sacrificed to to a [[:en:obe:kt:falsegod|false god]].
