====== Jeremiah Chapter 36 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Why did Yahweh want Jeremiah to write his words on a scroll?

A. Yahweh wanted Jeremiah to write his words on a scroll so that the people of Judah might listen to all the disasters which Yahweh intended to bring on them and turn from their wicked path so Yahweh could forgive their sin. [36:1-3]

Q? What did Jeremiah command Baruch to do?

A. Jeremiah commanded Baruch to take the scroll upon which Baruch had written Jeremiah's dictation and read Yahweh's words from the scroll in the hearing of the people in his house and in the hearing of all of Judah. [36:4-6]

Q? What was Jeremiah's purpose for having Baruch read the scroll?

A. Jeremiah was hopeful that each person would turn from their wicked way. [36:7-8]

Q? What happened during the fast which was proclaimed in honor of Yahweh?

A. Baruch read Jeremiah's words aloud in Yahweh's house in the hearing of all the people. [36:9-10]

Q? What happened after Micaiah saw all the officials in the secretary's room in the king's house?

A. Micaiah told them about all the words that he had heard Baruch read aloud in the people's hearing. [36:11-13]

Q? What did Jehudi say to Baruch?

A. Jehudi said, "Take the scroll in your hand which you were reading in the people's hearing and sit down and read it to them" [36:14-15]

Q? What happened after Baruch read from the scroll?

A. The men who heard Baruch read from the scroll decided they must report all the words to the king. [36:16]

Q? What advice did the officials give to Baruch after he acknowledged writing Jeremiah's words on the scroll?

A. The officials said to Baruch, "Go, hide yourself and Jeremiah. Do not let anyone know where you are." [36:17-19]

Q? How did the king respond to the reading of the scroll by Jehudi?

A. The king would cut off the scroll with a knife and throw it into the fire in the brazier until all of the scroll was destroyed. [36-20-23]

Q? What impact did the words on the scroll have on the king and his servants?

A. They were not frightened nor did they tear their clothes. [36:24]

Q? What did the king do when several men urged him not to burn the scroll?

A. The king did not listen to them. [36:25]

Q? How did Yahweh choose to overcome the destruction of the scroll by the king?

A. Yahweh instructed Jeremiah to have Baruch write on a new scroll all the words that were on the original scroll as a rebuke to Jehoiakim, king of Judah. [36:27-29]

Q? What words did Yahweh use to rebuke Jehoiakim, king of Judah?

A. Yahweh said, "No descendant of yours will ever sit on the throne of David, your corpse will be thrown out into the heat of day and the frost of night, and I will punish your descendants for the iniquity of you all." [36:30-31]
