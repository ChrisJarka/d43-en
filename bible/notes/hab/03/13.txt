===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Habakkuk continues to describe his vision of Yahweh.

  * **You went out for the salvation of your people** - The word "salvation" can be translated with a verb. AT: "You went out to save your people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **your anointed one** - "the one whom you have anointed." Here this phrase refers to one whom Yahweh has chosen. Possible meanings are 1) "the people you have chosen" or "the nation you have chosen" or 2) "the king you have chosen."
  * **You shatter the head of the house of the wicked to expose the base of the neck** - This sentence is difficult to translate. Possible meanings are 1) Yahweh destroying the leader of a wicked people is spoken of as if Yahweh the leader were the head of a family which Yahweh severs from its neck or 2) Yahweh destroying the leader of a wicked nation is spoken of as if that leader were the head or roof of a building that Yahweh completely destroys. See the following notes for comments on individual aspects of the metaphor. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the house of the wicked** - Possible meanings are 1) this is a metaphor in which the wicked nation is spoken of as if it were a house. AT: "the wicked house" or "the wicked nation" or 2) the word "house" is a metonym for "family" and here represents a people group. AT: "the wicked family" or "the wicked people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the head of the house of the wicked** - Possible meanings are 1) the leader of the wicked nation is spoken of as if he were the head of a house, with the head of a house being a metaphor for the roof or 2) the leader of the wicked people is spoken of as if he were their head. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **to expose the base of the neck** - "to lay bare the base to the neck." Possible meanings are 1) "the base" refers to the bottom part of the neck, and this is all that is left after the head is cut off. AT: "to leave only the base of the neck" or 2) "the base" refers to the bottom part of the body and "to expose" refers to stripping one of clothing. AT: "to strip him from the lower body to the neck" or 3) "the base" refers to the bottom part of a building and "the neck" refers to the top part of the building after the roof is gone. AT: "to destroy from bottom to top" or "to destroy from top to bottom"
