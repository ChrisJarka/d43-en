====== Introduction to Obadiah ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

=== Outline of the Book of Obadiah ===

  - God's judgments on the nation of Edom
    - The destruction of Edom is predicted (1:1–4)
    - The complete desolation and destruction of the city is predicted (1:5–9)
    - The hatred of Edom for Israel (1:10–14)
    - Edom and the other nations will be punished (1:15–16)
  - God promises to rescue his people; they will live in their land again (1:17–19)
  - Yahweh promises to restore his kingdom (1:20–21)

=== What is the Book of Obadiah about? ===

The Book of Obadiah is a prophecy that Obadiah transmitted from God to the people of Israel. Obadiah said that Yahweh would punish the nation of Edom for capturing Israelites fleeing from Judah and for handing them over to the Babylonians. Obadiah also prophesied that Yahweh would restore the city of Jerusalem and would rule as divine king once more. 

=== How should the title of this book be translated? === 

Translators may decide to translate this traditional title, "The Book of Obadiah," in a way that is more clear to the readers. One possible title is "The sayings of Obadiah." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|Translate Names]]). 

=== Who wrote the Book of Obadiah? ===

Obadiah probably wrote this book. We know nothing more about the writer than that his name in Hebrew means, "Servant of Yahweh."

===== Part 2: Important Religious and Cultural Concepts =====

=== What was Edom's relationship to Israel? ===

Obadiah refers to Edom as Israel's "brother." This is because the Edomites were descendants of Esau, while the Israelites were descendants of Jacob, Esau's brother. This fact made Edom all the more guilty for having mistreated the Israelites.

===== Part 3: Important Translation Issues =====

=== How should one translate the concept of pride? ===

Pride is a major theme in the Book of Obadiah. The nation of Edom is accused of being proud, of thinking that they were more powerful than Yahweh and that he was not able to punish them.
