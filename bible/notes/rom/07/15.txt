===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **For that which I do, I do not really understand**  - Paul is expressing his frustration and disappointment in himself for not doing what he knows is right.There appears to be a struggle between the choices of obedience and disobedience which prevents him from making right choices.  Alternate translation: "I am not sure why I do some of the things that I do". (See: [[:en:ta:translate:hyperbole|Hyperbole]])
  * **For what I do not want, I do, and what I hate, I do**  - This can be translated as: "The things that I know that are not good are the things that I do."
  * **But if I do that which I do not want** - Alternate translation " 'On the other hand' or 'however' if I do what I know is not good."
  * **I agree with the Law**  -Alternate translation: "The Law is good and lets me know I am not pleasing God."
