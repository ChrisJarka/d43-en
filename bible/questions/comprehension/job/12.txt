====== Job Chapter 12 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What does Job say about himself?

A.Job said that he has understanding and is not inferior to his friends. [12:1-3]

Q? What do Job's neighbors think of him now that he has misfortune.

A.Job's neighbors think that his is like a laughingstock because of his misfortune.[12:4â€“5]

Q? What does Job knows about himself?

A.Job knows that he is one who called upon God and was answered by God. 12:4]

Q? Who does Job think should teach his friends?

A.Job wants them to ask the beasts, birds, earth and the fish of the sea to teach his friends. [12:7-8]

Q? What does Job question about the animals?

A. J\ob questions if any animal whould not know that the hand of Yayweh has given them life and has also given the breath to all mankind. [12:9-10]

Q? What does Job think about his friends memorable saying?

A.Job thinks that his friends sayings are proverbs made of ashes and their defenses are made of clay. [12:12]

Q? What does Job want his friends to do so that he can speak?

A.Job wants his friends to hold their peace so that he can speak. [12:13]

Q? What will Job defend even though he may take his own life or be killed by God?

A. Even if he kills himself or is killed by God, Job will defend his ways before God. [12:14-15]

Q? Why does Job think he should be acquitted before God?

A. Job thinks he should be acquitted because he is not a godless man. [12:16]

Q? Does Job say about presenting his defense to his friends?

A. Job says that he has set his defense to show his innocence. [12:18]

Q? What does Job say he will do if he is proven wrong?

A. Job says that if he is proven wrong, he would silently give up his life. [12:19]

Q? What two things does Job ask of God?

A. Job asks God to withdraw God;s oppressive hand from Job and to not let God's terrors bring fear to Job. [12:20-21]

Q? What does Job ask God to tell him?

A. Job asks God to let him know Job's transgressions and sins. [12:23]

Q? Who does Job think has hidden his face from Job and treated Job like an enemy?

A. Job thinks that God has hidden his face from Job and treats him like an enemy. [12:24]

Q? What does Job say God has done to him?

A. Job says that God has written down bitter things against him, made him inherit the iniquities of his youth, put his feet in stocks., and watches where Job goes. [12:26-27]

Q? What does Job say about himself?

A. Job says that he is like a rotten thing that wastes away and garments that moths have eaten. [12:28]
