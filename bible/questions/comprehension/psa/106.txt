====== Psalms Chapter 106 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? For what two reasons does the writer give thanks to Yahweh? 

A. Give thanks to Yahweh, for he is good, for his covenant faithfulness endures forever. [106:1]

Q? When the writer asks, "Yahweh, when you show favor to your people," what does he ask for himself? 

A. The writer asks Yahweh to "Call me to mind... help me when you save them." [106:4]

Q? When the writer says, "Yahweh... show favor to your people," what does he go ahead to say about that favor? 

A. "...I will see the prosperity of your chosen, rejoice in the gladness of your nation, and glory with your inheritance." [106:5]

Q? When the writer says, "We have sinned, we have done wrong, and we have done evil," who does he say they are acting like? 

A. "We have sinned like our ancestors..." [106:6]

Q? What two places does the writer mention as he describes how, "...our fathers did not appreciate your marvelous deeds?" 

A. The writer mentions "...your marvelous deeds in Egypt... they were rebellious at the sea, the Red Sea." [106:7]

Q? When the writer says, "Nevertheless, he saved them for his nameâ€™s sake," why does he say Yahweh did that? 

A. "...he saved them for his nameâ€™s sake so that he might reveal his power." [106:8]

Q? The writer says that Yahweh "saved them from the hand of those who hated them," but what happened to their adversaries? 

A. "But the waters covered their adversaries; not one of them survived." [106:11]

Q? The writer says, "They had insatiable cravings in the wilderness," what then did they challenge in the desert? 

A. "They challenged God in the desert." [106:14]

Q? What does the earth do when, "the camp... became jealous of Moses and Aaron, the holy priest of Yahweh?" 

A. "The earth opened and swallowed up Dathan and covered the followers of Abiram." [106:17]

Q? What did the people worship at Horeb? 

A. "They made a calf at Horeb... the image of a bull that eats grass." [106:20]

Q? The writer says that God, "...would have decreed their destruction," but what happened to turn away his anger? 

A. "Moses, his chosen one, intervened with him in the breach." [106:23]

Q? The writer says that God, "raised his hand and swore to them," that he would do what? 

A. God "swore to them that he would let them die in the desert, scatter their descendants among the nations..." [106:26]

Q? The writer says, "Then Phinehas rose to intervene..." and although we are not told here what he did, how do we know God was pleased?

A. "The plague subsided." [106:30]

Q? The writer says the people, "...angered Yahweh at the waters of Meribah, and Moses suffered because of them." What happened to Moses?

A. "They made Moses bitter, and he spoke rashly." [106:33]

Q? The text says, "They did not destroy the nations as Yahweh had commanded them, but they mingled with the nations and learned their ways." What did they then do that defiled them? 

A. "They sacrificed their sons and their daughters to demons." [106:37]

Q? The writer says that Yahweh was angry with his people and "He handed them over to the nations."  How did those nations feel about them?

A. Yahweh handed them over to, "those who hated them and ruled over them." [106:41]

Q? The writer says that Yahweh paid attention to their distress and heard them, for what reason? 

A. "He called to mind his covenant with them and relented because of his covenant faithfulness." [106:45]

Q? What is the purpose the writer has in mind when he asks, "Save us, Yahweh, our God. Gather us from among the nations?" 

A. "So that we may give thanks to your holy name and glory in your praises." [106:47]
