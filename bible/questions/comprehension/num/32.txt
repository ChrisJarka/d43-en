====== Numbers Chapter 32 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Why did the descendants of Reuben and Gad want the land of Jazer and Gilead to be given to them as a possession?

A. The descendants of Reuben and Gad wanted the land of Jazer and Gilead to be given to them because it was a wonderful place for livestock. [32:1-5]

Q? Why did Moses question the descendants of Reuben and Gad when they made their request to be given the land of Jazer and Gilead?

A. Moses questioned the descendants of Reuben and Gad because he thought the people of Israel might be discouraged from going into the land that Yahweh had given them if the descendants of Reuben and Gad settled down before they crossed the Jordan. [32:6-7]

Q? When had Moses previously seen this kind of discouragement set in among the people of Israel?

A. Moses saw this kind of discouragement set in among the people of Israel after Yahweh sent them to examine the land in the Valley of Eshcol and as a result they refused to go into the land that Yahweh had given them. [32:8-9]

Q? Why did Yahweh take an oath that none of the men of Israel from twenty years old and up except Caleb and Joshua would see the land which he had swore to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob?

A. Yahweh took this oath because he was angry that the men of Israel had not completely followed him. [32:10-12]

Q? What did Yahweh do because he was angry with the men of Israel?

A. Yahweh made them wander around in the wilderness for forty years until the generation who had done evil was destroyed. [32:13]

Q? What promise did the descendants of Reuben and Gad make to Moses if he would allow them to settle down and build fences for their cattle and cities for their families?

A. The descendants of Reuben and Gad promised to be armed to go with Israel's army and not return to their houses until every man of Israel had obtained their own inheritance. [32:16-19]

Q? What final decision did Moses make concerning the request from the descendants of Reuben and Gad to settle down in the land before the Jordan?

A. Moses told them that if they would go to war with the men of Israel and drive out the enemies in the land, then they could return to the land they possessed and be guiltless towards Yahweh and Israel, but if they did not, then they will have sinned against Yahweh. [32:20-23]

Q? What did the descendants of Reuben and Gad agree to do?

A. They agreed to settle their cattle and families in the land before the Jordan and then join the men of Israel in battle. [32:25-27]

Q? What would happen if the descendants of Reuben and Gad did not cross over and fight with the men of Israel to take the land Yahweh had given to them?

A. If the descendants of Reuben and Gad did not cross over and fight with the men of Israel, then they would not receive the land in Gilead, but would acquire their possessions in the land of Canaan. [32:28-30]

Q? What was the decision which the descendants of Reuben and Gad and the half tribe of Manasseh confirmed in response to the leaders of the people of Israel?

A. The descendants of Reuben and Gad and the half tribe of Manasseh told the leaders, "We will cross over armed before Yahweh into the land of Canaan, but our possessed inheritance will remain on this side of the Jordan." [32:31-33]
