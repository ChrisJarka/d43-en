====== Litotes ======

This module answers the question: What are litotes?\\
Before you start this module have you learned about:
  * [[en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_intro|Figures of Speech]]

===== Description =====

Litotes is a strong statement made by negative phrases to strengthen a positive meaning.  Litotes are made to say that the opposite idea is not true.

=== Reason this could be a translation issue ===\\ 
Some languages do not use litotes and would fail to understand that the statement is strengthen but would see it as weakened.
----
===== Examples from the Bible =====
**For you yourselves know, brothers, our visit to you was __not in vain__,** 1 Thessalonians 2:1\\
By using litotes, Paul emphasized that his visit with them was very worthwhile.\\ 
\\ 
**All things were made through him. __Without him__ was __not anything__ made that has been made.** John 1:3 (WEB)\\
By using litotes John emphasized that the Son of God created absolutely everything.\\ 

**Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old he will __not turn away__ from that instruction.**\\ 
The proverb uses the litotes __not turn away__ to emphasize that he will continue to obey that instruction. 

===== Translation Suggestions =====
  * Use the litotes if it would be understood.
  * If it would not be clear, say emphatically what __is__  true.
----
===== Examples of Translation Strategies Applied =====

In languages that the litotes would not be clear:\\ 

**For you yourselves know, brothers, our visit to you was not in vain,** (1 Thessalonians 2:1)\\ 
For you yourselves know, brothers, our visit to you did much good.\\ 
 
**Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old he will not turn away from that instruction.** (Proverbs 22:6) \\ 
Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old he will continue to follow that instruction.\\
