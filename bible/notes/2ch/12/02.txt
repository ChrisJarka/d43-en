===== translationNotes: =====

  * **It happened** - This phrase is used here to mark where the action starts. If your language has a way for doing this, you could consider using it here.
  * **in the fifth year of King Rehoboam** - This refers to the fifth year of Rehoboam's reign as king. AT: "in the 5th year that Rehoboam was king" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_ordinal]])
  * **Shishak, king of Egypt, came up against Jerusalem** - "Shishak, king of Egypt" is a metonym for Shishak along with the Egyptian army. AT: "Shishak, king of Egypt, and his army with him, came up against Jerusalem" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Shishak** - This is the name of a man. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **came up against** - This is an idiom that means marched against or attacked. AT: "came to attack" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **twelve hundred chariots and sixty thousand horsemen** - "1,200 chariots and 60,000 horsemen" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_numbers]])
  * **Soldiers without number** - This exaggeration means that there were more soldiers than a person could easily count. AT: "Many soldiers" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **Libyans, Sukkites, and Ethiopians** - These are people from Libya, Sukki, and Ethiopia. The location of Sukki is uncertain, but it may be a region in Libya. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_unknown]])
