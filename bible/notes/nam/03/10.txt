===== Translation Notes: =====


    * **Yet**  - refers to a continuation of the comparison of Nineveh and Thebes from Nahum 3:8-9. Possible meanings: "nevertheless"(See: UDB) or "even so"
    * **Thebes** - refers to Thebes, Egypt, the once capital of Egypt; an important city which was invaded by the Assyrians in 664 B.C.
    * **she... her**  - refer to Thebes. 
    * **dashed in pieces** - compares the children to something that could be thrown and broken apart.  I is used to say they were thrown down and killed. (See: [[en:ta:translate:hyperbole]])
    * **threw lots** - means that the invaders gambled or played a game in which the winner took a prize, an official, or "great man," of Thebes. 
    * **were bound in chains** - refers to being put into slavery.  Alternate translation: "became slaves" (See: [[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])
    * **You**  - refers to Nineveh.
    * **you will try to hide; you also will look for a refuge from your enemy** - The word "refuge" refers to a safe place.  These two phrases are saying the same thing.  The thought is mentioned twice to add emotion to the words.  (See: [[en:ta:translate:parallelism]])
