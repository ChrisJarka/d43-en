====== Psalms Chapter 137 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What did the writer say they thought about and where were they when they sat down and wept?

A. They were by the rivers of Babylon thinking about Zion. [137:1]

Q? Where did they hang their harps and what did the captors require them to sing?

A. Harps were hung on the trees and their captures required them to be happy and sing songs of Zion. [137:2-4]

Q? What does the writer say will happen if he forgets Jerusalem and thinks about Yahweh no more?

A. He says his right hand will forget her skill and his tongue will cling to the roof of his mouth. [137:5-6]

Q? What did the writer want Yahweh to remember about the Edomites?

A. He wanted Yahweh to remember that on the day Jerusalem fell the Edomites said, "Tear it down, tear it down to its foundations". [137:7]
