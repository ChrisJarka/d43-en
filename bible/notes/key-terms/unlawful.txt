====== unlawful ======

===== Definition: =====

The term "unlawful" or "not lawful" is used in the New Testament to refer to doing something that is against Jewish laws. It is also used once to refer to doing something against Roman laws.

  * The Jewish religious leaders had added extra rules to the laws that had been given to Moses, such as rules for what was allowed to be done on the Sabbath day.
  * In the book of Acts, Peter stated that eating "unclean" foods or associating with Gentiles was unlawful for him .
  * Other ways to translate “unlawful” can be: “not permitted” or “not according to God's law” or “not conforming to our laws.”

(See also: [[:en:bible:notes:key-terms:lawful]], [[:en:bible:notes:key-terms:sabbath]], [[:en:bible:notes:key-terms:moses]])
