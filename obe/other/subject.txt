====== subject, be subject to, put in subjection ======

===== Facts: =====

One use of the term "subject" means to be under the authority of someone and to obey that authority.

  * <font 14px line-height: 19.6px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>A person who is under the authority of a king or other ruler, is said to be the "subject" of that ruler.</font>
  * <font 14px line-height: 19.6px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>To "be subject to" is a command that means "obey" or "submit to."</font>
  * In the Bible, the phrase "put in subjection to"  refers to God causing everything, whether living or nonliving,  to submit to his authority and obey him.
  * Note: A different meaning of "subject" which means to be the one who is the topic or the focus.
