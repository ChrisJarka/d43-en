===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Epicureans-**  They believed all things were formed by chance. They also believed that the gods were too busy being blessed and happy to be bothered with the governance of the universe.
  * **Stoics**-They believed that freedom comes from resigning oneself to fate. They rejected a personal loving God and the resurrection.
  * **philosophers**-People who talk about what is real or important in lifeâ€‹.
  * **some said**  - "some people said" (see Synecdoche)
  * **babbler-**  The word was used to refer to birds picking up seeds as food. It is a negative term used for gossipers. The philosophers said Paul is someone who has bits of information which were not worth listening to.
  * **Others said**  - "other people said" (see Synecdoche)
  * **He seems to be a preacher **- "He seems to be a proclaimer" or "He seems to be in a mission of spreading the message"
  * **Strange gods**  - not in the sense of "odd", but in the sense of "foreign", that is, non-Greek/Roman gods.
