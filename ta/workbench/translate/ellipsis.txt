====== Ellipsis ======

This module answers the question: What is ellipsis?\\
In order to understand this module, it would be good to read
  * [[en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_intro|Figures of Speech]]

[[Summary of 100 words or less]]

===== Description =====

Ellipsis is where words are left out of the sentence because it is not needed to be understood. The information that is omitted has usually already been stated in a preceding sentence or phrase. (See also: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])

  * **the wicked will not stand in the judgment, nor sinners in the assembly of the righteous** (Ps 1:5)

  * **The sun rises from the one horizon and crosses the sky to the other** (Ps 19:6)

**Reason this is a translation issue**\\
Readers who see incomplete sentences or phrases may not be able to supply the missing information.

===== Examples from the Bible =====

**when the blind man was near, Jesus asked him, "What do you want me to do for you?" He said, "Lord, __that I might receive my sight__."** (Luke 18:40-41)\\
The man answered in an incomplete sentence because he wanted to be polite by giving Jesus only as much information as necessary.

**He makes Lebanon skip like a calf __and Sirion like a young ox__.** (Psalm 29:6)\\
The writer wants his words to be few and to make good poetry.

===== Translation Strategies =====
If your language can use ellipsis consider using it. These strategies are for if your language cannot use ellipsis.
  - Add the missing words to the incomplete phrase or sentence.

===== Examples of Translation Strategies Applied =====

1. Add the missing words to the incomplete phrase or sentence.

  * **when the blind man was near, Jesus asked him, "What do you want me to do for you?" He said, "Lord, __that I might receive my sight__."** (Luke 18:40-41)
    * when the blind man was near, Jesus asked him, "What do you want me to do for you?" He said, "Lord, I want you to heal me that I might receive my sight."

  * **He makes Lebanon skip like a calf __and Sirion like a young ox__.** (Psalm 29:6)
    * He makes Lebanon skip like a calf, and he makes Sirion skip like a young ox.
