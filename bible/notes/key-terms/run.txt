====== run, running ======

===== Definition: =====

Literally "run" means to use one's legs to move quickly, at a faster pace than walking.

  * <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>There are also figurative uses for this primary meaning of "run":</font>      * <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>​"run in such a way as to win the prize" – compares running a race and persevering in the Christian life.</font>
      * <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>"run in the path of your commands" – means to gladly and energetically pursue obedience to God's instructions</font>
      * <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>"run after other gods" – means to persist in worshiping other gods</font>
      * <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>"I run to you to hide me" – means someone is quickly going to God for refuge and safety</font>
  * Secondary meanings of "run":
      * Flowing liquids, such as water, tears, blood and sweat, are spoken of as "running."
      * Borders are said to "run" along a river
      * Rivers and streams can "run dry," which means that they no longer have water in them.
      * The days of a feast can "run their course" which means they have passed by or finished.
