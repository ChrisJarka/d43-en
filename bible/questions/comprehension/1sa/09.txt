====== 1 Samuel Chapter 09 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What were the outstanding physical features of Saul, the son of Kish?

A. Saul was a handsome young man who was taller from his shoulders upward than any of the other people. [9:1-2]

Q? What was the outcome when Kish asked his son Saul to go and look for their lost donkeys?

A. Saul took one of the servants and passed through the hill country of Ephraim, through the land of Shalishah, the land of Shaalim, and the land of the Benjamites without finding the lost donkeys. [9:3-4]

Q? What did Saul say to his servant?

A. Saul told his servant that they should go back or his father would worry more about them than the donkeys. [9:5]

Q? What different idea did the servant present to Saul?

A. The servant told Saul that they should seek after a man of God in the city who might be able to tell them where they should go on their journey. [9:6]

Q? What did the servant have in his posession to give to the man of God so he would tell them which way to go?

A. The servant told Saul that he had with him a fourth of a shekel of silver to give to the man of God. [9:7-8]

Q? What did Saul and his servant inquire of the young women who were coming out to draw water?

A. Saul and his servant wanted to know if the seer was in the city so they could seek the knowledge of God's will. [9:9-11]

Q? Why was the seer coming to the city on that day?

A. The seer was coming to the city on that day because the people were sacrificing at the high place and the people would not eat until after the seer would bless the sacrifice. [9:12-13]

Q? What did Yahweh reveal to Samuel about the role which Saul would play in the life of Israel?

A. Yahweh told Samuel that on the next day he would send a man to be annointed prince over Israel and he would save Yahweh's people from the hand of the Philistines. [9:15-16]

Q? What invitation did Samuel give to Saul after he revealed his identity as the seer Saul was seeking?

A. Samuel invited Saul to go up and eat with him at the high place and he would tell him everything that was on his mind. [9:17-19]

Q? How did Samuel suprise Saul by what he told him?

A. Samuel surprised Saul by telling him that even though he was from a clan which was the least of the tribe of Benjamin, all the desires of Israel were on him and his father's house. [9:20-21]

Q? What did Samuel tell Saul to do after the cook had given him a thigh that had been raised in sacrifice?

A. Samuel told Saul that he should eat it because it had been kept until the appointed time for him. [9:23-24]

Q? What did Samuel say to Saul after they had come down from the high place into the city?

A. Samuel told Saul, who was on a rooftop, to get up so he could send him on his way. [9:25-26]

Q? Why did Samuel tell Saul he must stay on the outskirts of the city for awhile?

A. Samuel told Saul to stay awhile in the outskirts of the city so that Samuel could announce the message of God to him. [9:27]
