====== Discourse Information ======


===== Description =====

Discourse markers are like cohesion markers in that they join sentences together on a higher level in connecting events, episodes, into a coherent story line.

(Translation Academy: [[:en:ta:discourse:home]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_cohesion]])

===== Template =====

The following information should be included in the notes:

  - **Text** - the phrase with the discourse marker from the ULB in bold
  - Clarifying translation - (optional)
  - **Explanation** - the meaning of the marker unless clear in the alternate translation
  - **Alternate translation** - Point the translator to how his language handles the discourse marker
  - **TA link** - unless already in the notes for the chunk

  * **(1) ULB text**  - **(3)** Meaning. **(4)**  Suggestion to use the way their language expresses this topic. **(5)** (See: topic)


===== Bible Examples =====

Field reference numbers are included for clarity purposes only. They are omitted in the actual note.


=== Acts 12:20 ===

Now Herod was very angry with the people of Tyre and Sidon. 

  * **(1) Now Herod** - **(2)** “At that time, Herod.” **(3)** The word “Now” marks a change in the story timeline. **(4)** If your language has a way of expressing a change in a story, use it here. **(5)** (See: Discourse)
