===== Translation Notes: =====


    * **my...I**  - Personal pronouns in verses 1-3 refer to Paul. (See: [[en:ta:translate:singularpronoun]])
    * **Therefore, my beloved brothers whom I long for,**  - "My dear brothers, I love you and I long to see you." 
    * **my joy and crown,**  - Paul is using metonymy to express how happy he is when he thinks about the Philippians and the reward God will give Paul for helping them to believe in Jesus. This can be translated: "You give me joy because you have believed in Jesus and you are my reward for my work." (See: [[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])  
    * **in this way stand firm in the Lord, beloved friends.**  - "So keep on living for the Lord in the way that I have taught you, dear friends."
    * **Euodia and Syntyche**  - Two women who were believers and helped Paul in the church at Philippi.
    * **I am pleading with Euodia, and I am pleading with Syntyche,**  - Paul uses hyperbole to express how much he wants these ladies to work out their disagreement. This can be translated: "I beg Euodia and I beg Syntyche," (See: [[en:ta:translate:hyperbole]])
    * **be of the same mind in the Lord.**  - "agree with each other in the Lord."
    * **Indeed, I am entreating you too, my true yoke-fellow,**  - "Yes, I ask you too, my true fellow-worker,"
    * **these women, they"  - Refers to Euodia and Syntyche.
    * **Clement**  - A man who was a believer and worker in the church at Philippi.
    * **fellow-worker**  - This term refers to believers who helped other people in Philippi to believe in and live for Jesus.
    * **whose names are in the book of life.**  - "whose names are written in the Book of Life."
