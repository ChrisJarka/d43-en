====== Adjectival Noun ======


===== Description =====

There are two type of Adjectival Nouns.

  - A noun that acts like an adjective and describes another noun.
  - An adjective that is used as a noun to refer to a category of people.

==== Adjectives as Nouns ====

=== Rut:02:20 ===

"And Naomi said to her daughter-in-law, 'May he be blessed by Yahweh, who has not left off his loyalty to the living and to the dead.'"

  * **(1) to the living and the dead**  - **(3)** Naomi and Ruth were the "living" and Naomi's husband and sons were the "dead."  **(4)** These adjectival nouns could be translated as "to the people who are still alive and to the people who have already died."  **(5)** (See: Adjectival Noun)

=== Rut:01:08 ===

"May Yahweh show loyalty toward you, as you have shown loyalty toward the dead and toward me."

  * **(1) toward the dead and to me**  -  **(3,4)** This could be translated by replacing the adjectival noun "dead" with the verb "die": "to your husbands who died, and to me."  **(5)** (See: Adjectival Noun)

===James 4:6===

But God gives even more grace, which is why the scripture says, "God resists the proud, but gives grace to the humble."

  * **(1) the proud** - (4) "proud people" or “the prideful people.” **(5)** (See: Adjectival Noun)
  * **(1) the humble** - (4) “humble people”
