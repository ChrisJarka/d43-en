===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Apples of gold ... silver is a word ... situation** - Most translations translate this metaphor as a simile and change the order of the phrases. The goodness of "a word spoken at the right time" is spoken of as if it were the physical beauty of "apples of gold in settings of silver. AT: "A word ... situation is like apples of gold ... silver" or "A word ... situation is beautiful, like apples of gold ... silver" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **Apples of gold in settings of silver** - The ancient Israelites did not know the apples that most people know today. Possible meanings are 1) another physical fruit with a golden color that someone has placed on a silver plate or bowl or 2) the words "apples of gold" are a metaphor for a tasty fruit that someone has served at the right time, like when the eater is especially hungry or thirsty, or 3) something that a sculptor has made out of gold and put a silver frame around. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **a word** - a message
  * **A gold ring ... fine gold is a wise rebuke ... ear** - Most translations translate this metaphor as a simile and change the order of the phrases. The value and importance of "a wise rebuke" is spoken of as if it were either the gold's 1) money value or 2) physical beauty. AT: "A wise rebuke ... ear is like a gold ring ... fine gold" or "A wise rebuke ... ear is valuable, like a gold ring ... fine gold" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **listening ear** - The ear is a synecdoche for the whole person. AT: "person who is willing to listen" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
