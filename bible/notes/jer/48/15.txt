===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Moab will be devastated and its cities attacked** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "The enemy army will devastate Moab and attack its cities" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **its finest young men have gone down to the place of slaughter** - The best men of Moab going to battle and being killed is spoken of as if they were animals taken to a place to be slaughtered. AT: "their fine young men will all be slaughtered" or "the enemy army will kill all of the finest men of Moab" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **This is the king's declaration** - Here "king" refers to Yahweh. This can be stated in first person. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:jer:46:18|46:18]]. AT: "This is what I, the king, declare" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person]])
  * **Moab's disaster is soon to happen; calamity is hurrying quickly** - These two sentences mean basically the same thing and emphasize that Moab's destruction will happen very soon. AT: "Moab's enemies will destroy her very soon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **calamity is hurrying quickly** - Something terrible happening soon is spoken of as if calamity were something that could move quickly to a place. AT: "terrible things will happen very soon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the strong staff, the honored rod, has been broken** - Here the words "staff" and "rod" represent the power and political support that Moab provided to other nations. This can be stated in active form. AT: "Moab's enemies have ended her great power" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
