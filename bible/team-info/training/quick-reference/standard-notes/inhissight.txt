====== in his sight/eyes ======

===== Description =====

The phrase "call on the name of Yahweh" is used in the Old Testament. The phrase "call on the name of the Lord" is used in the New Testament.

===== Standard Notes =====


  * **in his sight/eyes** - AT: "where he could see" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **in his sight/eyes** - AT: "and he saw it" or "and he knew it"(See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **in his sight/eyes**  - AT  "in his judgment" or "which he considers to be ..."  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **in the sight/eyes of all nations** - AT: “so that all the surrounding people-groups knew”  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])


When writing the note, choose either "sight" or "eyes." \\ 
Replaces “his" and "he" with the phrase that fits the context.  \\ 
Make sure that the tense fits the context. \\
