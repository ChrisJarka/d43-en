====== Sample Idiom Notes ======

Sample notes from Jim's revisions:

  * **in the name of our Lord Jesus** - an idiomatic expression for coming together to worship Jesus Christ (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Is it** - This is an idiom in which "it" refers to the remaining content of the sentence. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom|Idiom]])
  * **a child born at the wrong time** - This is an idiom by which Paul may mean that he became a Christian much later than the other apostles. Or he perhaps means that, unlike the other apostles, he did not witness Jesus' three-year-long ministry. AT: "someone who missed the experiences of the others." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **until he has put all his enemies under his feet** - This symbolizes the fact that Christ will conquer all his enemies. AT: "until God has made all of Christ's enemies bow down to him." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **no one would be shaken** - to be "shaken" is to be frightened or made afraid. "no one would be frightened away from trusting in Christ" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **I could no longer stand it** - "I could not longer wait patiently." Here Paul was describing his own emotions. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **if you stand firm in the Lord** - To "stand firm" is to   remain faithful to one's belief in the Lord. AT: "if you continue to trust in the Lord" (UDB) (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **mind your own business** - This means to not interfere with other people's concerns. AT: "attend to your own needs." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **you are all sons of the light...not sons of the night** - Here "sons of the light" refers to followers of Christ. "Sons of the night" refers to all others who follow the world. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **and have turned away from these things** - Here "turned away" is an idiom that means they have stopped doing what God has commanded. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **redeem the time** - "be wise about what you do" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **that the eyes of your heart may be enlightened** - The phrase "eyes of your heart" expresses one's ability to gain understanding. AT: "that you may gain understanding and be enlightened." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **seated him at his right hand** - "seated Christ at God's right hand" or "seated Christ in the place of honor" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **you once walked** - This expresses the behavior of how people live. AT: "you once lived." (see [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **so that the whole body grows and builds itself up in love** - "so believers can help each other grow in love" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **words...that are useful for meeting needs** - "words...that encourage and help other people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **deep respect and trembling** - The idiom "deep respect and trembling" uses two similar words to emphasize the idea of honor. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **flesh and blood** - This expression refers to people, not spirits who do not have human bodies. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **remember the poor** - "take care of the needs of the poor" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **I have you in my heart** - "I love you very much" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **So what** - Paul is stating that it is not important why some people are teaching about Jesus. AT: "I do not care" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Do not look out for your own needs** - "Do not think only about how to get the things you need" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **Gentiles, who do not have the law...are a law to themselves** - The phrase "law to themselves" is an idiom that means that these people naturally obey God's laws. AT: "have God's laws already inside them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **bears witness to them, and their own thoughts either accuse or defend them to themselves** - Here "bears witness" refers to the knowledge they gain from the law that God has written in their hearts. AT: "tells them if they are disobeying or obeying God's law" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **rest upon the law, rejoice proudly in God** - The phrase "rest upon the law" is an idiom. Paul speaks of relying on the law as if one could actually lean on it for support. AT: "and you rely on the law of Moses and rejoice proudly because of God"  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **They have all turned away** - This is an idiom that means the people do not even want to think about God. They want to avoid him. AT: "They have all turned away from God" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Be of the same mind toward one another** - AT: "Agree with one another" or "Live in unity with each other" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **The God of peace will soon crush Satan under your feet** - The phrase "crush under your feet" refers to complete victory over an enemy. AT: "Soon God will give you peace and complete victory over Satan" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **lazy bellies** - an idiom for people who do nothing but eat too much food. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **It was not written on tablets of stone, but on tablets of human hearts** - The word "stone" pictures something that does not change. The phrase "human hearts" is used because they are soft and able to change. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **in the sight of God** - God's understanding of the writer's truthfulness is referred to as God being able to see them. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **has blinded** - AT: "prevented understanding" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **the light** - The word "light" refers to the truth. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **He has shone** - The word "shone" means to create light and refers to God creating understanding. AT: "he has given understanding." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **jars of clay** - The writer is using this phrase to refer to a human body. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **for an eternal weight of glory that exceeds all measurement** - The glory of Paul and Timothy is referred to as a weight so heavy that it cannot be measured. This is another way to say they will be greatly honored for what they have done. AT: "to be greatly honored in heaven forever." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **watching for** - This phrase refers a person wanting and expecting something to happen. AT: "wanting." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **for things that are seen** - This refers to possessions obtained during life. AT: "possessions" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **the earthly dwelling** - AT: "our physical body" (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **we have a building from God. It is a house not made by human hands, but an eternal house, in heaven** - God will provide a new, eternal body in which we will live. (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **For in this tent we groan** - AT: "For in this earthly body we struggle" (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **our heart is wide open** - AT: "we love you freely" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **tied together with** - "teamed up with" or "have a close relationship with" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **that you are in our hearts, for us to die together and to live together** - AT: "that we love you enough to live or die with you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **all the rest** - This expression means the remainder or everyone else. AT: "all the others." (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **take note of him** - "publicly identify that person" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **those who call on the Lord** - Here "call on the Lord" is an idiom that means to trust and worship the Lord. AT: "those who worship the Lord" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **They will be tickling their hearing** - This is an idiom that means the people will enjoy listening to new and different doctrines. AT: "In this way, they will always be looking for something new and different to learn" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
