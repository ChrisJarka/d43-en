====== Jeremiah Chapter 10 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What is the word the Yahweh is announcing to the house of Israel?

A. Do not learn the ways of the nations, and do not be dismayed by the signs in the heavens, for the nations are dismayed by these. [10:1-2]

Q? Why are the customs of idolatrous people worthless?

A. These idols are like scarecrows in a cucumber field, for they can say nothing, they have to be carried, for they cannot take any steps at all. [10:3,5]

Q? Why should the people not fear the idols?

A. They cannot bring about evil, nor are they able to do anything good. [10:5]

Q? How does the prophet describe Yahweh?

A. There is no one like Yahweh, who is great and his name is great in power. [10:6]

Q? Why does Yahweh deserve to be feared?

A. For there is no one like him among all the wise men of the nations or all their royal kingdoms. [10:7]

Q? Why are the rulers of the nations all the same?

A. They are brutish and stupid, disciples of idols that are nothing but wood. [10:8]

Q? But what is Yahweh?

A. He is the living God and eternal king. [10:10]

Q? How do the earth and the nations respond to Yahweh?

A. The earth quakes at his anger, and the nations cannot endure his anger. [10:10]

Q? How will the prophet speak to them?

A. He will speak to them like this, "The gods that did not make the heavens and earth will perish from the earth and from under these heavens." [10:11]

Q? What did the maker of earth do?

A. He in his power established dry land in his wisdom and spread the heavens in his understanding. [10:12]

Q? What does Yahweh make and bring up?

A. His voice makes the roar of waters in the heavens, and he brings up the mists from the ends of the earth; he makes lightning for the rain and sends out wind from his storehouse. [10:13]

Q? How does the prophet describe the idols poured in casts by the metalworker?

A. They are useless, the work of mockers; they will perish at the time of their punishment. [10:14-15]

Q? How is God, the portion of Jacob, different from these idols?

A. God, the portion of Jacob, is not like these, for he is the molder of all things. [10:16]

Q? What is Israel?

A. israel is the tribe of his inheritance. [10:16]

Q? What should the people who have been living under the siege do?

A. They should gather their bundle and leave the land. [10:17]

Q? What does Yahweh say he is about to do?

A. He is about to  throw the inhabitants of the land out this time; he will cause them distress, and they will find it to be so. [10:18]

Q? What did the woeful prophet say because of his broken bones and his infected wound?

A. Surely this is agony, but he must bear it. [10:19]

Q? Why is there no longer anyone to spread out his tent or to raise up his tent curtains?

A. They have taken his children away from him, so they no longer exist. [10:20]

Q? Why does the prophet say the shepherds have become stupid?

A. They do not seek Yahweh, so they have no success; all their flocks are scattered. [10:21]

Q? What is coming?

A. A great eathquake is coming from the land of the north to make the cities of Judah into ruins, hideouts for jackals. [10:22]

Q? What does the prophet tell Yahweh that he knows?

A. He knows that a man's path does not come from himself; no person walking directs his own steps. [10:23]

Q? How does the prophet ask Yahweh to discipline him?

A. He asks Yahweh to discipline him with justice, but not in Yahweh's anger, lest Yahweh destroy him. [10:24]

Q? On whom does the prophet ask Yahweh to pour his fury?

A. He asks Yahweh to pour his fury on the nations that do not know him and on the families that do not call on his name. [10:25]

Q? Why should they receive Yahweh's fury?

A. They should receive Yahweh's fury for they have devoured Jacob and consumed him so as to completely destroy him and demolish his habitation. [10:25]
