===== Translation Notes: =====

Continuation of metaphor in which Tyre is compared to a great ship in verses 3-11 but is now being destroyed in verses 26-36.

  * **<font 14px line-height: 15.6800003051758px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>Your rowers have brought you to many waters</font>    **- This may refer to the leaders of Tyre who endangered the people.
  * <font 14px line-height: 15.6800003051758px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>** The eastern wind has broken you in the heart of the seas  **</font>    - "Eastern wind" may refer to a destructive power or a symbol of God's power and indicates that Tyre is being destroyed in the middle of the seas.
  * Verse 27 describes a ship that is being sunk and may refer to the city of Tyre being destroyed.
  * **the ones who strengthen seals**  - "Ship workers"
  * **Men of war**  - "soldiers" or "army"
  * **<font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>in the day of your overthrow</font>    **- "at the time of destruction"
