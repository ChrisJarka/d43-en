====== Jeremiah Chapter 14 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What do the servants do after searching for water, when they return unsuccessful?

A. The servants return unsuccessful; they cover their heads ashamed and dishonored. [14:3]

Q? How does the doe treat her young because there is no grass?

A. The doe leaves her young in the fields and abandons them, for there is no grass. [14:5]

Q? What does Jeremiah ask Yahweh to do even though our iniquities testify against us?

A. Even though our iniquities testify against us, Yahweh, act for the sake of your name. [14:7]

Q? How does Jeremiah describe the hope of Israel?

A. Hope of Israel, the one who saves Israel in the time of distress. [14:8]

Q? What does Yahweh tell Jeremiah not to do for this people, since Yahweh is not pleased with them?

A. Yahweh said to Jeremiah, "Do not pray for good on behalf of this people." [14:11]

Q? From where does Yahweh tell Jeremiah the deceitful words of the false prophets come?

A. The false prophets' deceitful visions and useless and deceitful divination come from their own hearts. [14:14]

Q? What does Yahweh say will happen to the people to whom the false prophets prophesied?

A. The people to whom the false prophets prophesied will be thrown out in the streets of Jerusalem. [14:16]

Q? What will be seen when one comes to the city?

A. If I come to the city, then see, there are those diseased by famine. [14:18]

Q? What does Jeremiah admit to Yahweh is the iniquity of our ancestors?

Q? What does Jeremiah give as a reason to hope in Yawhweh?

A. Jeremiah says that Judah hopes in Yahweh, for he has done all these things. [14:22]
