====== Synecdoche ======


===== Description =====

Synecdoche is a figure of speech in which 1) the name of a part of something is used to refer to the whole thing, or 2) the name of a whole thing is used to refer to just one part of it.

===== Bible Examples =====

Field reference numbers are included for clarity purposes only. They are omitted in the actual note.

=== Gen:22:18 ===

"Through your offspring all the nations of the earth will be blessed, because you have obeyed my voice."

  * **(1) obeyed my voice**  - **(3)** God is calling attention to his voice when he is actually referring to himself. **(4)** It can be translated as "obeyed me". **(5)** (See: Synecdoche)

=== Luk:24:16 ===

"But their eyes were prevented from recognizing him."

  * **(1) Their eyes were prevented from recognizing him**  - **(2)** "Their eyes were kept from recognizing Jesus."  **(3)** The men's ability to recognize Jesus is spoken of as their eyes' ability to recognize him. **(4)** It can also be translated as "They were kept from recognizing him" or "Something prevented them so they could not recognize him." **(5)** (See: Synecdoche)

=== Act:15:07 ===

"Brothers, you know that a good while ago God made a choice among you, that by my mouth the Gentiles should hear the word of the gospel, and believe."

  * **(1) by my mouth** - **(3)** Peter uses this expression to refer to himself. **(4)** It can be translated as "by myself" or "by me". **(5)** (See: Synecdoche)
