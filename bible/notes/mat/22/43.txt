===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **How then does David in the Spirit call him Lord**  - Jesus is using a question to make a point. <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>(See: Rhetorical Question)</font>
  * **The Lord said to my Lord**  - The first Lord refers to God the father and the second Lord refers to the Messiah. David spoke of the Messiah as the Lord. (Psalm 110:1) This could be translated as "The Lord said to my master,"
  * **at my right hand **- "at a place of honor and importance" <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>(See: Metaphor)</font>
  * **Till I put your enemies underneath your feet.**  - "Until I, God, conquer your enemies." <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/inherit;;inherit font-size: 14px; line-height: 21.7777786254883px;;;inherit>(See: Idiom)</font>  <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/inherit;;inherit font-size: 14px; line-height: 21.7777786254883px;;;inherit></font>
