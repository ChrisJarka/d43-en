===== Translation Notes: =====

Jesus continues to talk to the crowds about John the Baptist.

  * Jesus continues talking about John the Baptist.
  * **But what did you go out to see **- This continues the series of rhetorical questions about John the Baptist. (See: Rhetorical Question)
  * **But what did you go out to see - a prophet? Yes, I say to you **- The plural pronoun "you" is referring to the crowd in both cases.
  * **This is he **- "John the Baptist is he"
  * **he of whom it was written**  - The pronoun "he" refers to "my messenger" in the next phrase.
  * **it was written **- The next phrase is a quotation from Scripture.
  * **I am send my messenger**  - The writer of this Old Testament prophecy is quoting what God said. The word "I" here refers to God, the original speaker of the prophecy.
  * **before your **- The messenger was sent to prepare the way for the coming of the Messiah. The singular pronouns "you" and "your" refer here to the Messiah.
