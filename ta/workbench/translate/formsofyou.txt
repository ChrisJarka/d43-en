====== Forms of 'You' ======

==== Singular, Dual, and Plural ====

Some languages have more than one word for ‘you’ depending on how many people the word 'you' refers to. If they are referring to one person, they use the ‘singular’ form of ‘you’. If they are referring to more than one person, they use the ‘plural’ form of ‘you.’ Some languages also have a ‘dual’ form for speaking to two people, and some have other forms for speaking to three or four people.

==== Formal and Informal ====

Some languages have more than one form of 'you' based on the relationship between the speaker and the person he is talking to. People use the **formal** form of 'you' when speaking to someone who is older, or who has higher authority, or who they do not know very well. People use the **informal** form when speaking to someone who is not older, or does not have  higher authority, or who is a family member or close friend.
