====== sign ======

===== Definition: =====

A sign is an object, event, or action that communicates a special meaning. Signs often prove that something is true.

  * <font 14px line-height: 15.6800003051758px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>A sign may be something that people do or something that God does. It may also be something that God commands people to do.</font>
  * After the flood that covered the earth, God made the rainbow as a sign to tell people that he would never again destroy all life on earth with a flood.
  * The miracles that the prophets did were signs that proved they were truly sent from God.
  * God commanded the Israelites to circumcise their sons as a sign of his covenant with them.
  * The baby Jesus lying in a feeding trough was a sign that would indicate to the shepherds that he was the Messiah the angel had told them about.
  * The miracles that Jesus performed were signs that proved that he was truly the Messiah.
  * Judas kissed Jesus as a sign, or signal, to the religious leaders that Jesus was the one that they should arrest.
  * The miracles that the apostles performed were signs that showed that God had sent them and that what they were teaching was true.
  * Depending on its context, "sign" could be translated as: "signal," "symbol," "mark," "evidence," "proof," or "gesture."

(See also: [[:en:bible:notes:key-terms:miracle|miracle, wonder, sign]], apostle, Messiah, covenant, circumcise, circumcision other)
