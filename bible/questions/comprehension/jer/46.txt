====== Jeremiah Chapter 46 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What was the prophesy given to Jeremiah concerning the nation of Egypt?

A. Egypt was to get their shields,horses,spears and armor (army) ready and go foward to fight. [46:1-4]

Q? What did Yahweh see in this prophesy?

A. He saw the Egyptian soldiers running for safety but defeated at the Euphrates River. [46:5-6]

Q? What is the Egyptain army compared to?

A. The army is like the Nile River when it floods and destorys cities and inhabitants. [46:7-8]

Q? What is the vengeance of Yahweh?

A. It is the enemy being devoured by the sword like a sacrifice to Yahweh by the Euphrates River. [46:10]

Q? What does Yahweh tell Egypt to do, but it is useless?

A. They are to go to Gilead for medicine but it is useless to cure them. The nations have heard of their disgrace. [46:11-12]

Q? What did Yahweh tell Jeremiah that Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon was about to do?

A. That Babylon was going to attack the land of Egypt. [46:13-14]

Q? Who has the power to knock down Egypt?

A. Yahweh will defeat Egypt through Nebuchadnezzar's soldiers. [46:15-17]

Q? What does Yahweh say Egypt needs to do?

A. He says Egypt is to get ready for captivity. [46:18-19]

Q? What word pictures does the writer use to describe Egypt?

A. Egypt is a beautiful young cow, the hired soldiers are like a fattened bull, another work picture is Egypt hisses like a snake. [46:20-22]

Q? Who will kill the soldiers of Egypt?

A. They will be conquered by people from the northeast (Babylon).[46:23-24]

Q? What does Yahweh say about Egypt?

A. Yahweh is going to punish Egypt by giving them into the hand of Nebuchadnezzar, but someday Egypt will be inhabited as in previous days. [46:25-26]

Q? What does Yahaweh say he will do for his people Israel?

A. He will bring them out of captivity, return them to their land where they will find peace and be secure, bring destruction to the nations where they were scattered, discipline them justly, but not destroy them completely. [46:27-28]
