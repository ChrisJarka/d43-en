===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Howl, O gate; cry, O city** - This refers to the people. These phrases mean the same thing and are used for emphasis. Alternate translation: "Weep bitterly at the city gates, you people of Philistia." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]])
  * **you will melt away** - "you will grow weak with fear"
  * **O Philistia** - Alternate translation: "you people of Philistia" or "O Philistines"
  * **For out of the north comes a cloud of smoke** - This describes a very powerful army that quickly moves toward the city. Isaiah compares the dust that goes in the air as they march to a cloud of smoke. This emphasizes that the army is terrifying and they have the power to destroy. (See: [[en:ta:translate:metaphor]])  
  * **and there is no straggler in his ranks** - "and no soldier will get tired in the king's army" or "and every soldier in the king's army will be ready to fight"
  * **How then will one answer the messengers of that nation?** - Isaiah uses a question to teach the people of Israel. Alternate translation: "This is what we must tell the messengers of Philistia." (See: [[en:ta:translate:rhetoricalquestion]])
  * **Yahweh has founded Zion** - "Yahweh has created Zion"
  * **and in her** - "and in Jerusalem"
  * **the afflicted of his people** - "those who are distressed" or "those who are suffering"
