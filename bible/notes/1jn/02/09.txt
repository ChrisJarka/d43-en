===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **He who says** - "Anyone who says" or "The person who says." This does not refer to a specific person.
  * **in the light** -  This is a metaphor that can be translated as "doing what is right" or "behaving like Christ" (UDB). (See: [[en:ta:translate:metaphor]])
  * **in the darkness** - This is a metaphor that can be translated as "doing what is evil."
  * **remains in the light** - "is doing what is right" or "behaving like Christ"
  * **there is no occasion for stumbling in him** - The word "stumbling" is a metaphor that mean to fail spiritually or morally. This could be translated as "and will never fail to do what is pleasing to God."
  * **in the darkness and walks in the darkness** - This is a doublet that is bringing attention to how evil it is to hate a fellow believer. This could be translated as "doing what is evil and does not have a relationship with Christ." (See: [[en:ta:translate:doublet]])
  * **he does not know where he is going** - This is a metaphor that can be translated as "he does not even know he is doing what is evil."
  * **the darkness has blinded his eyes**  - Here "the darkness" is personified as someone who has hurt the eyes of another person so they can no longer see.  And, here "eyes" are a metonymy which refers to the ability to understand God's truth. This could be translated as "sin has made it impossible for him to understand the truth." (See: [[en:ta:translate:personification]] and [[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])
