===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]]

  * **Why are the nations in rebellion, and the peoples conspire in vain?** - These two clauses have similar meanings, and the "nations" and "peoples" refer to the same group of people. AT: "Why do the nations rebel and conspire in vain?" (See [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]]) 
  * **Why are the nations in rebellion, and the peoples conspire in vain?** - This question is used to show surprise that the people are doing something so wrong and foolish. AT: "The nations are rebelling and plotting against God in vain." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **the nations ... the peoples** - This may refer to the leaders of the nations and peoples. AT: "the rulers of the nations" ... "the leaders of the peoples" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **The kings of the earth take their stand together and the rulers conspire together** - These two clauses have similar meanings. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **take their stand together** - Preparing to fight against someone is spoken of as if they were standing against him. AT: "prepare to revolt together"  or "unite and prepare to fight" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Let us tear off the shackles ... throw off their chains** -  The people of other nations speak of Israel's rule over them as if it were shackles and chains.  AT: "We should free ourselves from their control; we should not let them rule over us any longer!" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
