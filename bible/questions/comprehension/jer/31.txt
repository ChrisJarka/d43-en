====== Jeremiah Chapter 31 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What is Yahweh's declaration?

A. Yahweh's declaratrion is, "I will be the God of all the clans of Israel, and they will be my people." [31:1]

Q? Why did the people of Israel who survived the sword find Yahweh's favor in the wilderness?

A. The people of Israel found favor because Yahweh loved them with an everlasting love and drew them to himself with covenant faithfulness. [31:2-3]

Q? What is the good news that Jeremiah shared with virgin Israel?

A. The good news is that Yahweh will build them up so they will go out with happy dances and plant vinyards again on the mountains of Samaria. [31:4-5]

Q? Why does Yahweh tell Jacob to shout for joy?

A. Jacob should shout for joy because Yahweh has rescued the remnant of Israel. [31:7]

Q? What will Yahweh do for the remnant of Israel?

A. Yahweh will gather them from the farthest parts of the earth and lead them as they make their pleas. [31:8-9]

Q? How has Yahweh ransomed Israel?

A. Yahweh has ransomed Israel by gathering her up and keeping her as a shepherd keeps his sheep. [31:10-11]

Q? How will Israel celebrate being ransomed by Yahweh?

A. Israel will rejoice over Yahweh's goodness and their lives will become like a watered garden. [31:12]

Q? What is a Yahweh's declaration?

A. Yahweh's declaration is, "My people will fill themselves with my goodness." [31:14]

Q? Why is there wailing and bitter weeping in Ramah?

A. There is wailing a bitter weeping in Ramah because Rachel is weeping for her children who no longer live. [31:15]

Q? Why does Yahweh say there is hope for Israel's future?

A. There is hope for Israel's future because Yahweh has declared that their descendants will return inside their borders. [31:16-17]

Q? Why does Yahweh declare that he will have compassion on Ephraim?

A. Yahweh declares he will have compassion because Ephraim was ashamed, humiliated and sorry for their sins. [31:18-20]
