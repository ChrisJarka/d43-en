====== may it never be, God forbid, absolutely not ======

===== Definition: =====

These three phrases are used to emphasize that something is not true or that there is a strong desire for something to never happen.

  * <font 14px line-height: 13.0666666030884px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>Often these terms are used in response to a rhetorical question.</font> <font small line-height: normal;/arial, sans-serif;;inherit;;inherit>(See: [[:en:ta:figuresofspeech:</font> <font small line-height: normal;/arial, sans-serif;;inherit;;inherit>rhetoricalquestion|rhetoricalquestion]])â€‹</font>
  * When used by Paul, this is the strongest way of saying that what he is speaking about can never happen or must never happen.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * T<font 14px line-height: 13.0666666030884px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>he literal text is, "May this never come to be" or "May it be far from God that I should do this!"</font>
  * The term absolutely not could be translated as, "That is completely impossible!" or "Of course that is not true!"
  * If Paul says, "may it never be that I should sin," he means it is his fervent desire and wish that he not sin. This could be translated as, "May God help me to never do that!"
