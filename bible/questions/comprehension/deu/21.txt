====== Deuteronomy Chapter 21 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What should happen if someone who has been killed is found lying in a field and it is not known who attacked him?

A. The elders and judges should measure to determine the nearest city to the killed man so the elders of that city can break the neck of a heifer who has never borne a yoke in a valley with running water. [21:1-4]

Q? How did Yahweh want the people of Israel to respond to the words of his priests who had been chosen to serve him and bless the people?

A. Yahweh wanted the people of Israel to accept the advice of his priests as the verdict in every dispute and in cases of assault. [21:5]

Q? What ritual must the elders of the city nearest to the killed man perform so that this bloodshed will be forgiven?

A. The elders of the city must wash their hands over the heifer whose neck was broken in the valley and say, "Our hands have not shed this blood nor have our eyes seen it." [21:6-9]

Q? What did Moses tell the people of Israel to do when they went out to battle against their enemies and Yahweh would give them victory?

A. When Yahweh would give the people of Israel victory in battle, they were told to take their enemies away as captives. [21:10]

Q? How was an Israelite man to respond to the women who were taken captive?

A. If he saw a beautiful woman who he wanted to take for his wife, then he was to bring her home where she would shave her head, cut her nails, take off the clothes she was wearing, and remain in mourning for her father and mother for a month before she would become his wife, but if he would take no delight in her, he must let her go rather than sell her for money. [21:11-14]

Q? How must the man who has a wife he loves and a wife he hates, who has borne him his firstborn son, distribute what he owns to his heirs?

A. This man must acknowledge the firstborn son of the hated wife by giving him a double portion of all that he possesses. [21:15-17]
