===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **The vision of Isaiah**  - "This is what God showed Isaiah"
  * **Amoz** - Amoz was the father of Isaiah. Translate his name as needed into your language. (See: [[:en:ta:translate:translatenames]])
  * **that he saw concerning** - "about"
  * **Judah and Jerusalem** - "Judah" refers to the southern kingdom of Israel. "Jerusalem" is its most important city. "Judah" and "Jerusalem" are metonyms that refer to the people who live in these areas. This can be translated as "those living in Judah and Jerusalem." (See: [[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])
  * **in the days of** - This is an idiom that refers when a king ruled. It can be translated as: "in the years of" (UDB) or "during the rule of." (See: [[en:ta:translate:idiom]])
  * **Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah**  - These are all names of men.
  * **kings** - "rulers" or "leaders"
  * **Hear** - This marks the beginning of the message God gave Isaiah.
  * **Hear, O heavens, and listen, O earth** - Isaiah personifies “Heavens” and “earth” and he is talking to them like a person who has seen the people of Judah and Jerusalem sinning. (See: [[en:ta:translate:personification]])
  * **Yahweh**  - This is the name of God that he revealed to his people in the Old Testament. See the Important Term page concerning how to translate this.
  * **I have nourished and brought up children** - This is a metaphor that can be translated as "I have taken care of the people living in Judah like they were my children." (See: [[en:ta:translate:metaphor]])
  * **I...me** - Here these words refer to God.
