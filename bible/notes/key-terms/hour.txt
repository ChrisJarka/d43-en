====== hour ======

===== Definition: =====

In the Bible, the term "hour" is often used to tell what time a certain event took place. This term is also used in several figurative ways.

  * <font 14px line-height: 19.600000381469727px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>The Jews counted daylight hours starting at sunrise, so "the ninth hour" means "three in the afternoon" in the present-day system.</font>
  * <font 14px line-height: 19.600000381469727px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>Night hours were counted starting at sunset, so</font>    "the third hour of the night" means "nine in the evening."
  * Since the Jews' way of referring to time is not exactly the same as the way time is referred to in every culture, time expressions could be translated with phrases such as "around nine" or "about six o'clock."
  * Nighttime hours were also referred to in terms of a certain "watch," which was a period of time of 3 to 4 hours when a city guard would be on duty to guard the city.
  * <font 14px line-height: 19.600000381469727px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>The following are some figurative uses of "hour":</font>      * Sometimes "hour" refers to a regular, set time to do something such as, "the hour of prayer."
      * T<font 14px line-height: 19.600000381469727px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>he phrase, "in that very hour" means "at that moment" or "at that time."</font>
      * When the "hour had come" for Jesus to suffer and be put to death, this meant that it was the appointed time that God had decided long ago that this would happen.
      * When used figuratively, the term "hour" can be translated as "time" or "moment" or "appointed time."

(See also: watch, watchman other)
