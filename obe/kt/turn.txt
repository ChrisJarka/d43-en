====== turn, turn away ======

===== Definition: =====

To "turn" or "turn away" means to physically change direction or to cause something else to change direction.

  * To "turn back" means to "go away" from someone or something or to "cause someone to go away."
  * To "turn back to God" means to "start worshiping God again."
  * To "turn away from" idols means to "stop worshiping" idols.
  * To "turn toward" someone means to "face" that person directly.
  * To "turn and leave" or "turn his back to leave," it means to turn around and go away.
  * When God "turned away" from his people, it meant that he "stopped protecting" or "stopped helping" them (especially when they rebelled against him).
  * To "turn into" can also mean to "become" or to "cause to become." For example when Moses' rod "turned into" a snake it meant that it had become a snake.


===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * Depending on the context, "turn" can be translated as "change direction" or "go" or "move."
  * In many contexts, it can also mean to "cause (someone) to do something."
  * To "turn back to God" means to "start worshiping God again."
  * To "turn away from" idols means to "stop worshiping" idols.
  * When enemies "turn back," it means they "retreat."
  * Used figuratively, when Israel "turned to" false gods, it meant that they had "started to worship" them. When they "turned away" from idols, it meant they had "stopped worshiping" them.
  * In the context of when God "turned away from" his people, this phrase could be translated as "stopped protecting" or "stopped helping."
  * The phrase "turn the hearts of the fathers to their children" could be translated as, "cause fathers to care for their children again."
  * The expression, "turn my honor into shame" could be translated as, "cause my honor to become shame" or "dishonor me so that I am shamed" or "shame me (by doing what is evil) so that people no longer honor me."
  * "I will turn your cities into ruin" could be translated as, "I will cause enemies to destroy your cities."
  * To "turn something away" means to "cause something to go away."
  * The term "return" could be translated as, "go back" or "give back" or "start doing again."
