====== Nehemiah Chapter 09 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What were the people of Israel doing as they gathered together, fasted, wore sackcloth, and had put dust on their heads?

A. They confessed their sins and the evil actions of their ancestors, read from the book of the law of Yahweh, bowed down and called out to Yahweh. [9:1-4]

Q? What did they say about Yahweh?

A. They said that he was Yahweh alone. He had made heaven, the earth, the seas, and all that was in them and gave them life. [9:5-6]

Q? What else did they say of Yahweh concerning Abram?

A. They said that he had chosen Abram, changed his name to Abraham, found his heart faithful, and made the covenant with him to give his descendants the land. [9:7-8]

Q? How did they say that Yahweh kept his promise to them?

A. They told how Yahweh brought their forefathers out of Egypt, showed signs and wonders against Pharaoh and his people, and saved the forefathers at the Red Sea. [9:9-10]

Q? What did they say that Yahweh did for the Israelites at the Red Sea?

A. He parted the sea so they could pass through on dry ground, and then he brought the sea back onto those that were pursuing them. [9:11]

Q? What other ways did they say Yahweh took care of the Israelites?

A. He led them by a pillar of cloud during the day, a pillar of fire during the night, and at Mount Sinai, he gave them his commandments. [9:12-13]

Q? What else did they say that Yahweh provided to their forefathers?

A. He made his holy sabbath known to them, gave them bread from heaven, water from a rock, and told them to possess the land he had promised them. [9:14-15]

Q? How did they say the Israelites and their ancestors acted, and how did God react?

A. They were disrespectful, stubborn, refused to listen, rebelled, and appointed a leader to return them to their slavery, but God did not abandon them. [9:16-19]

Q? How did they say that God provided for their forefathers for forty years in the wilderness?

A. He gave his Spirit to instruct them, food and water, their clothes did not wear out and their feet did not swell. [9:20-21]

Q? What did they say happened after their forefathers were in the wilderness?

A. They said that Yahweh multiplied their families, told them to go in and possess the land he had promised them, and subdued the Canaanites. [9:23-24]

Q? How did the Israelites live after taking possession of the land God had promised to them?

A. They took the land and all that was there for themselves and delighted in God's goodness. [9:25]

Q? What did they say that Yahweh did to their forefathers and ancestors after they became disobedient and rebelled against him?

A. He handed them over to their enemies, who made them suffer. [9:26-27]

Q? When the Israelites cried out to God, what did God do for them because of his great mercies?

A. He rescued them from their enemies many times because they would repeatedly do evil, be turned over to their enemies, cry out for God's mercy, and then refused to obey God's commands. [9:27-29]

Q? What happened to the Israelites and their ancestors because they acted wickedly, did not keep Yahweh's law, and did not listen to his warnings?

A. They became slaves in their own kingdom and had to give the kings, that Yahweh set over them, the rich yield from the land. [9:34-37]

Q? What did they do because of their great distress?

A. They made a firm covenant in writing with Yahweh. [9:38]
