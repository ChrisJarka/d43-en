====== subject, be subject to, put in subjection to ======

===== Facts: =====

The term "subject" refers to being under the authority of someone. The phrase <font 14px line-height: 19.6px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>"be subject to" is a command that means "obey" or "submit to."</font>

  * <font 14px line-height: 19.6px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>A person who is under the authority of a king or other ruler, is said to be the "subject" of that ruler.</font>
  * In the Bible, the phrase "put in subjection to" refers to God causing everything, whether living or nonliving, to submit to his authority and obey him.
  * <font 14px line-height: 19.6px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>To "subject someone to something" means to cause that person to experience something negative, such as punishment.</font>
  * Note: A different use of "subject" means to be the one who is the topic or the focus of something, such as the "subject of ridicule.".
