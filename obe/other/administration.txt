====== administration, administrator ======

===== Facts: =====

The term "administration" refers to the managing or governing of people, including enforcing rules to help a country or workplace be orderly and efficient. An "administrator" is a person who manages and governs people in this way.

  * Because of his wisdom, Daniel was appointed to be an administrator over the province of Babylon. At his request, three of his Jewish friends were also given positions of administration in the Babylonian government.
  * As administrators, these young men were in charge of making sure that the palace and other property of the king was well taken care of.
  * They also administered other affairs that probably included making sure that the people of Babylon followed the laws of the land.
  * In the New Testament, the term "administration" is used to refer to one of the gifts or graces of the Holy Spirit.
  * A person who has the spiritual gift of administration is able to lead and govern people as well as supervise the maintenance of buildings and other property.
  * Some ways to translate "administrator" could include: "governor" or "organizer" or "manager" or "ruler," depending on the context.
  * The term "adminstration" could be translated as, "governing" or "management" or "leadership." or "organization."
  * Expressions such as "in charge of" or "taking care of" or "keeping order" could possibly be part of the translation of these terms.
