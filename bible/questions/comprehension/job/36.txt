====== Job Chapter 36 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What does Elihu say to Job about God's righteousness when Elihu says a little more in defense of God?

A. Elihu says that his knowledge comes from far off and he acknowledges that righteousness belongs to his Maker. [36:1-3]

Q? What does Elihu, who claimes to be mature in knowledge, tell Job about God who is mighty?

A. Elihu tells Job that God is mighty, despises no one and is mighty in strength of understanding. [36:4-5]

Q? What does Elihu say that God does for those who are wicked and those who suffer?

A. Elihu says that God does not preserve the life of the wicked and he does what is right for those who suffer. [36:6]

Q? What does God not do for the righteous?

A. God does not withdraw his eyes from the righteous. [36:7]

Q? What does God reveal to those who are bound in chains and trapped in cords of suffering?

A. God reveals to those who are bound in chains and trapped in cords of suffering what they have done, their transgressions and how they behaved arrogantly. [36:8-9]

Q? What will happen to those who open their ears to God's instruction and turn back from their iniquity?

A. Those who open their ears to God's instruction and turn back from iniquity will worship God, spend their days in prosperity and their years in contentment. [36:10-11]

Q? What will happen to the people who do not listen to God's instruction and turn back from iniquity?

A. The people who do not listen and turn back from iniquity will perish by the sword and die because they have no knowledge. [36:12]

Q? What does Elihu say will happen to the godless person who stores up anger and does not cry out to God for help?

A. The godless person who stores up anger and does not cry out to God for help will die in their youth and their lives will end in disgrace. [36:13-14]

Q? How does Elihu say God uses affliction and oppression?

A. Elihu says that God uses affliction and oppression to rescue the afflicted and to open their ears. [36:15-16]

Q?What does Elihu say that God would like to do for Job?

A. Elihu says that God would like to draw Job out of his distress into a broad place. [36:16]

Q? What has laid hold on Job because he is full of the judgment on the wicked?

A. Judgment and justice have laid hold on Job because he is full of the judgment on the wicked. [36:17]

Q? What things could lead Job to deception and turn him aside from justice?

A. Wealth could lead Job to deception and a bribe could turn him aside from justice. [36:18]

Q? What things will not be able to help Job out of his distress?

A. Job will not be helped our of his distress through his wealth of by all the force of his strength. [36:19]

Q? Why does Elihu say thatJob is being tested by suffering?

A. Elihu says that Job is being tested by suffering so that he will stay away from sinning. [36:21]

Q? What cannot be said about God who is exalted in his power and whose deeds are praised in song?

A. It cannot be said about God that he has committed unrighteousness. [36:22-24]

Q? What do people not understand about God even though they have see his deeds?

A. Even though people have looked on God's deeds, they do not understand God's greatness. [36:25-26]

Q? What is the question that Elihu asks about God who draws up drops of water that distill as rain and pour from the clouds in abundance on mankind?

A. Elihu asks if anyone can understand the extensive spread of the clouds and the thunder from his tent. 36:27-29]

Q? Why does Elihu say that God spreads lightning and covers the sea with darkness?

A. God spreads lightning around himself and covers the sea with darkness in order to feed people and give food in abundance. [36:30-31]

Q? What tells people and cattle of the coming storm?

A. The noise of the lightning bolts hitting their targets tell people and cattle of the coming storm. [36:32-33]
