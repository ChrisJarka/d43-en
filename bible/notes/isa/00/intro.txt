====== Introduction to Isaiah ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

**Outline of Isaiah**\\

  - Rebuke and promised restoration of Judah (Chapters 1-5)
  - Doom of many empires (Chapters 6-23)
  - Universal judgment and promise of redemption (Chapters 24-27)
  - Indictment of the Chosen people of Yahweh (Chapters 28-31)
  - Restoring the Davidic monarchy and promise (Chapters 32-33)
  - Judgment on Edom (the nations) and promise of restoration to the ransomed (Chapters 34-35)
  - Hezekiah and Sennacherib (Chapters 36-39)
  - Comfort promised, comfort, deliverance, and judgment (Chapters 40-45)
  - Prophecies against Babylon (Chapters 46-48)
  - Redemption of Servant of Yahweh (Chapters 49-55)
  - Ethics of Yahweh’s Kingdom (Chapters 56-59)
  - Restored Zion (Chapters 60-66)
 

**What is Isaiah about?**\\
Isaiah lived in Jerusalem and warned Judah about their coming destruction as a nation. The book record some of the [[:en:obe:kt:prophet|prophecies]] of Isaiah.


**How should the title of this book be translated?**\\ 
It is suggested that translators use a self-explanatory title or follow the regional or the national language versions of the Bible (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|Translate Names]]). "Isaiah" may also be called the "The book about Isaiah."

**When did Isaiah live?**\\
The prophet Isaiah wrote this great book about 740-700 BC, a time when the Assyrian empire was increasing in power. The period of Isaiah’s ministry is found in the history of 2 Kings 19-20. Isaiah sees the rising power of the Assyrian empire. At the time in which he lived, Babylon was not a major force, just an Assyrian province, but a hundred years later, Babylon was the dominating power in the entire region. Isaiah sees in his prophetic vision the rise and power of Babylonia (the nation) and the coming destruction of Israel.
