===== Translation Notes: =====


    * **people**  - The word "people" refers to the Israelites.
    * **him**  - the word "him" refers to the Chaldeans.
    * **all these people take up a parable** - The words "take up" is a picture of someone taking a thought or saying from someone else.  They are taking the words of someone else and repeating them. "all these people start repeating a parable"
    * **a taunting proverb** - A statement meant to make someone angry or point out their faults.
    * **For how long will you increase your the weight of the pledges you have taken**  - The word "pledges" refer to a promise note given for a debt that is owed.  The phrase "increase the weight" means 1) "increase the amount" or "increase the burden."  Owing money to the Chaldeans is compared to carrying something heavy which makes it hard to do anything. The people's debt to the Chaldeans continues to get greater. (See: [[en:ta:translate:metaphor
]]) This question is used to suggest to the Chaldeans that the burden of the people's debt is too much. "You should stop increasing the burden of debt on the people." (See: [[en:ta:translate:rhetoricalquestion]])
    * **Won't the ones gnashing at you rise up suddenly, and the ones terrifying you awaken** - This question was asked in order to make the Chaldeans think about the answer. "The ones who are angry at you will come against you, the ones you are afraid of will begin to attack" (See: [[en:ta:translate:rhetoricalquestion]]) The word "gnashing" refers to a person biting or pressing their teeth together to show anger or sorrow.
    * **rise up suddenly** The words "rise up" means to become more.  This means the people will come together into a group for more power and attack the Chaldeans. "come against you suddenly"
    * **plundered** - The word "plundered" refers to things that have been stolen or taken by force.
    * **on account** - "because"
