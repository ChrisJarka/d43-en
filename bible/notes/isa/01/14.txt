===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Your...your **-** ** <font 14px line-height: 19.6000003814697px;/Lato, sans-serif;;rgb(51, 51, 51) font-family: Lato, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 19.6000003814697px;;;inherit>Here these words refer to the people of Israel.</font>
  * **new moons and appointed feasts**  - This Synecdoche refers to set times to worship God. (see: Synecdoche)
  * **my - ** <font 14px line-height: 19.6000003814697px;/Lato, sans-serif;;rgb(51, 51, 51) font-family: Lato, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 19.6000003814697px;;;inherit>Here this word refers to God.</font>
  * **soul hates**  - God does not like the attitude of the people and the way they have defiled the feasts.
  * **they**  - here this word refers to the feasts
  * **me...I**  - Here these words refer to God.
  * <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px; text-indent: -42px;/Lato, sans-serif;;rgb(51, 51, 51) font-family: Lato, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21.7777786254883px; text-indent: -42px;;;inherit>** they are a burden to me; I am tired of enduring them **</font> - <font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/Lato, sans-serif;;rgb(51, 51, 51) font-family: Lato, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21.7777786254883px;;;inherit>This is a metaphor that can be translated as “They are a heavy load that God is tired of carying.” (See:</font> [[https://door43.org/en/ta/translate/metaphor|Metaphor]]<font 14px line-height: 21.7777786254883px;/Lato, sans-serif;;rgb(51, 51, 51) font-family: Lato, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21.7777786254883px;;;inherit>)</font>
  * **endure**  - This term is similar in meaning to “persevere" or "perseverance”.
