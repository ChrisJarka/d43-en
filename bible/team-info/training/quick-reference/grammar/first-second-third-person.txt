====== First, Second, or Third Person ======

===== Description =====

First person is how a speaker normally refers to himself. English has the first person pronouns ”I” and ”we”. (Also: me, my, mine, us, our, ours)

Second person is how a speaker normally refers to the person or people he is speaking to. English uses the second person pronoun ”you.” (Also: your, yours)

Third person is how a speaker refers to someone else. English has the third person pronouns ”he”, ”she”, ”it” and ”they”. (Also: him, his, her, hers, its, them, their, theirs.)

  - People normally use first person pronouns when referring to themselves.
      * The mother said to her young children, ”Stop fighting over the ball. Give it to me.”
  - But sometimes they use a noun instead of a pronoun.
      * The mother said to her young children, "Stop fighting over the ball. Give it to Mommy.
  - People normally use second person pronouns when referring to the person they are speaking to. But sometimes they use third person. (See Genesis 33:14 and Matthew 11:15 below.)

===== Usage =====

There are many places in the Bible where someone speaks about himself or herself in an unexpected form. The most common is speaking about oneself in the third person.

===== Bible Examples =====

Field reference numbers are included for clarity purposes only. They are omitted in the actual note.

=== Genesis 33:14 ===

"May my lord go ahead of his servant. I will travel more slowly, at the pace of the cattle that are before me, and at the pace of the children, until I come to my lord in Seir."

  * **(1) my lord** - **(3)** This is a polite and formal way for Jacob to refer to his older brother when speaking to him. **(4)** Alternate translations: “you” or "you, my lord." **(5)** (See: First, Second or Third Person)

  * **(1) his servant** - **(3)** This is a polite and formal way for Jacob to refer to himself when speaking to his older brother. **(4)** Alternate translations: “me” or "me, your servant."

=== Matthew 11:15 ===

"He who has ears to hear, let him hear."

  * **(1) He who has ears to hear, let him hear**  - **(3)** It may be more natural in some languages to use the second person: **(4)** "You who have ears to hear, hear." **(5)** (See: First, Second or Third Person)

=== Luke 17:25 ===

"But first he must suffer many things and be rejected by this generation."

  * **(1) But first he must suffer**  - **(3)**  The word "he" refers to the Son of Man which refers back to Jesus. Here, Jesus was speaking about himself as the "Son of Man." **(4)**  Alternate translations: "But first the Son of Man must suffer" or " but first I, the Son of Man, must suffer." (**5**) (See: First, Second, or Third Person)
