===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Then it came to pass** - "on" or "It came about" - This phrase is used here to mark the beginning of a new part of the story. If your language has a way for doing this, you could consider using it here.
  * **the word of Yahweh came to me** - "Yahweh spoke to Ezekiel" or "Yahweh gave Ezekiel a message".
  * **Son of man** - addressing Ezekiel.
  * **lift up** - "give" or "tell".
  * **You were like a young lion** - This portion begins a word of the Lord to the King of Egypt. "like a young lion" This simile means that the King, "you", was "implusive" or "acting without thinking" or "aggresive". (See:  Simile).
  * **among the nations** - This means as one of a group of nations.
  * **But you are like a sea monster within the seas** - "But you are like a monster in the water" (See: Simile).
  * **you burst forth with your rivers. And you stir up waters with your feet; you muddy their rivers!** - "breaking out, causing trouble, and ruining (or 'making dirty') their clear water". The word rivers could refer to canals dug by the Egyptians.
