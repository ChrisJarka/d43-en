====== Jeremiah Chapter 02 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What does Yahweh command Jeremiah to proclaim in the ears of Jerusalem?

A. Yahweh commands Jeremiah to say that Yahweh says he has called to mind on your behalf the covenant faithfulness in your youthfulness, your love at the time when we were engaged, when you went after me in the wilderness, the land that was not sown. [2:2]

Q? How does the prophet describe Israel in relation to Yahweh?

A. Israel is holy to Yahweh, the first fruits of the harvest. [2:3]

Q? What does Yahweh say to the house of Jacob?

A. Yahweh says this: "What did your fathers find wrong with me, that they went far from following me? That they went after useless idols and became useless themselves?" [2:4-5]

Q? What did the people do when God brought them to the land of Carmel?

A. They defiled the land; they made God's inheritance an abomination. [2:7]

Q? For whom did the prophets prophesy?

A. The prophets prophesied for Baal and walked after unprofitable things. [2:8]

Q? For what have Yahweh's people exchanged their glory?

A. Yahweh's people have exchanged their glory for what cannot help them. [2:11]

Q? What is Yahweh's declaration?

A. Yahweh declares, "Shudder heavens, because of this! Be shocked and horrified." [2:12]

Q? Why does Yahweh declare this?

A. He declares this because his people have committed two evils against him; they have abandoned the springs of living waters by digging out cisterns for themselves, broken cisterns that cannot hold water. [2:13]

Q? What did young lions do against Israel?

A. They made a lot of noise and made his land a horror! [2:15]

Q? What happened to Israel's cities?

A. His cities are destroyed without any inhabitants. [2:15]

Q? When did the people do this to themselves?

A. They did this when they abandoned Yahweh their God, while he was leading them along the way. [2:17]

Q? What do their wickedness and faithlessness do?

A. Their wickedness rebukes them and their faithlessness punishes them. [2:19]

Q? What should they understand?

A. They should understand that it is wicked and bitter for them to abandon Yahweh their God, and to dread him no more. [2:19]

Q? What did the people say even after Yahweh boke their yoke and tore their fetters?

A. They said, "I will not serve!" [2:20]

Q? How does Yahweh describe their iniquity even if they clean themselves in the river or wash with strong soap?

A. Their iniquity is a stain before him. [2:22]

Q? To what animals does Yahweh compare them?

A. They are a swift camel running its own way; they are a wild donkey, accustomed to the wilderness, longing for life and panting after the useless wind. [2:23-24]

Q? How will the house of Israel be ashamed?

A. Like the shame of a thief when he is found, so the house of Israel will be ashamed. [2:26]

Q? What do they say to the tree and the stone?

A. They say to the tree, "You are my father," and to the stone, "You gave birth to me." [2:27]

Q? How many idols do they have?

A. Judah's idols equal their cities in number. [2:28]

Q? What is Yahweh's declaration?

A. All of you have sinned against me. [2:29]

Q? Why does Yahweh say he has punished their people in vain?

A. They would not accept discipline. [2:30]

Q? What have they done that Yahweh contrasts to a virgin never forgetting her jewelry nor a bride her veils?

A. His people have forgotten him for days without number. [2:32]

Q? What has been found on their clothes?

A. The blood that was the life of the innocent and the poor has even been found on their clothes. [2:34]

Q? What do they keep saying in spite of these things?

A. They keep saying they are blameless. Surely Yahweh's anger will turn from them. [2:35]

Q? How will they go out from there?

A. They will go out from there dejected, with their hands on their head, for Yahweh has rejected the ones whom they trusted,  so they will not be helped by them. [2:37]
