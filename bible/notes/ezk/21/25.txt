===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **whose day has come**  - "Your day has come" is an idiom that here means a day of judgment or punishment. It can also mean a day of joy or celebration if used in a positive context. (See: Idiom)
  * **whose - ** This pronoun refers to the wicked ruler of Israel.
  * **Remove the turban and take off the crown**  - The turban and crown represent the high status of the king.
  * **This is not the same! **- Without the crown, the king's point of view is different. Removing the crown lowers his status.
  * **I will make it a ruin!**  - The Lord Yahweh will destroy the kingdom of the wicked ruler.
  * **This also**  - Here the Lord Yahweh refers to the whole kingdom. It will lose its status as a nation.
  * **until the one who has right to it **- This refers to the future kingdom of the Messiah.
  * **Then I will give it to him **- The Lord Yahweh will give the kingdom to the Messiah.
