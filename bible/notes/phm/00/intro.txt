====== Introduction to Philemon ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

=== Outline of the Book of Philemon ===

  - Greeting (1:1-3)
  - Instructions concerning Onesimus (1:4-21)
  - Conclusion (1:22-25)

=== What is the Book of Philemon about? ===

Paul wrote this letter to a man named Philemon.  Philemon was a member of the church and also a slave owner. Paul wrote to him to ask that he would forgive his slave Onesimus who ran away and not punish him. Paul instructed Onesimus to return to Philemon and to be [[:en:obe:kt:reconcile|reconciled]] to his master. By Roman law, Philemon had the right to put Onesimus to death.  Paul uses persuasive arguments to encourage Philemon to accept Onesimus as a Christian brother and even suggests that Onesimus be allowed to serve alongside Paul.

=== Who wrote the Book of Philemon? ===

Paul from the city of Tarsus was the author and wrote this letter while in prison. He had been known as Saul in his early life, when as a Pharisee he was a determined persecutor of Christians. After he began to trust in Jesus Christ, he made a number of missionary journeys in order to preach the gospel among both Jews and non-Jews living outside the land of Israel.

=== How should the title of this book be translated? === 

Translators may choose to call this book by its traditional title, "Philemon." Or they may choose a clearer title, such as "Paul's Letter to Philemon," or "The Letter Paul wrote to Philemon." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|Translate Names]])

===== Part 2: Important Religious and Cultural Concepts =====

=== Does this letter condone the practice of slavery? ===

While Paul instructs Onesimus to return to his former master, this does not mean that slavery is an acceptable practice. Instead, Paul lessons focuses on serving God no matter the circumstances in which one finds themselves.

===== Part 3: Important Translation Issues =====

=== What are the major issues in the text of the Book of Philemon? ===

The following are the most significant textual issues in the Book of Philemon:\\

  * faith "in" Jesus (1:5). Some older versions read, faith "toward" Jesus

Translators are advised not to translate this following the ULB. However, if older Bible versions exist in their region follow older manuscripts, it is not wrong to translate in this way.
