====== First, Second, or Third Person ======

===== Description =====

First person is written from the speaker’s point of view. The pronoun “I” or “we” is used from the position of the speaker or writer.

Second person text tells what ‘you’ have done or ought to do.

Third person is told from the perspective of someone outside of the story. The pronouns “he”, “she”, “it” or “they” are used.

===== Usage =====

The following procedure can be used to create a note when it is unclear who the pronoun is referring to.

===== Template: First Person =====

Notes should follow this format:

  - identify phrase with pronoun
  - repeat phrase with pronoun and insert referent

=== Col 1:24 ===

Now I rejoice in my sufferings for you, and fill up in my flesh what is lacking of the afflictions of Christ for his body’s sake, which is the church.

  * **Now I rejoice**  - "Now I, Paul, rejoice"
===== Template: Second Person =====

Notes should follow this format:

  - identify phrase with pronoun
  - repeat phrase with pronoun and insert referent

=== Col 1:24 ===

Now I rejoice in my sufferings for you, and fill up in my flesh what is lacking of the afflictions of Christ for his body’s sake, which is the church.

  * **for you**  - "for you Colossian believers"
  * **for you**  - the Colossian believers
  * **for you**  - "you" refers to the Colossian believers
  * **for you**  - This refers to the Colossian believers.

=== Col 1:21 ===

And you also, at one time, were strangers from God and were his enemies in your mind and by your evil deeds.

  * **And you also**  - “And you Colossian believers also”.

===== Template: Third Person =====

Notes should follow this format:

  - identify phrase with pronoun
  - repeat phrase but substitute referent for pronoun

=== Col 1:18 ===

He is the head of the church, his body. He is the originating authority, the firstborn out of the dead ones, so he has first place among all things.

  * **he is the head**  - “Jesus Christ, the Son of God, is the head”
=== Col 1:13 ===

He has rescued us from the dominion of darkness and transferred us to the kingdom of his beloved Son.

  * **He has rescued us**  - “God the Father has rescued us”
===== Other Bible Examples =====

=== Luk:09:58 ===

"Jesus said to him, 'Foxes have holes, and the birds of the sky have nests, but the Son of Man has nowhere to lay his head.'"

  * **Son of Man**  - Jesus is speaking about himself in the third person. This could be translated as "I, the Son of Man." (See: [[:en:ta:translate:123person|First, Second or Third Person]])
=== Luk:15:04 ===

"Which one of you, if he has a hundred sheep and then loses one of them, will not leave the ninety-nine in the wilderness, and go after the lost one until he finds it?"

  * **Which one of you, if he has a hundred sheep**  - Since the parable starts with "Which one of you", some languages would continue the parable in the second person "if you have a hundred sheep." (See: [[:en:ta:translate:123person|First, Second or Third Person]])
=== Luk:17:25 ===

"But first he must suffer many things and be rejected by this generation."

  * **But first he must suffer**  - "But first the Son of Man must suffer." Jesus was speaking about himself in the 3rd person. (See: [[:en:ta:translate:123person|First, Second or Third Person]])
