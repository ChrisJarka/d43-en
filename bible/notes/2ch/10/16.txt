===== translationNotes: =====

  * **all Israel** - Here "Israel" â€‹is a metonym for the men of Israel. The word "all" is a generalization. AT: "the men of Israel" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **the king did not listen to them** - Here "listen to the them" represents caring about what they said or actually doing what they asked for. AT: "the king did not do what they had asked him to do" or "the king ignored them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **What share do we have in David? We have no inheritance in the son of Jesse** - The people use this question and statement to imply that since they do not belong to David's family, they are not obligated to support David's grandson, King Rehoboam. AT: "We have no share in David. We will not have anything to do with the descendants of the son of Jesse" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **What share do we have in David?** - Having a share in someone represents being his descendants and receiving the good things that descendants receive. AT: "We do not belong to the family of David" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] )
  * **We have no inheritance in the son of Jesse** - "Son of Jesse" is a metonym for David, a son of Jesse. Having an inheritance in someone represents being his descendants and receiving the good things that descendants receive. AT: "We will not receive any inheritance from the son of Jesse" or "We will have nothing to do with the his descendants" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Each of you should go back to his tent** - "Tents" is a metonym representing a people's homes. AT: "Go to your homes, people of Israel" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Now see to your own house, David** - Here "see to" is an idiom meaning to take care of something, and "house" is a metonym for David's lineage of power and prestige. AT: "Now take care of your own kingdom, descendant of David" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
