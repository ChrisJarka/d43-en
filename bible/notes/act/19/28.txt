===== translationNotes: =====

  * **When they heard** - "When the craftsmen heard"
  * **they were filled with anger** - This speaks of the people as if they were containers. Here "anger" is spoken of as if it were the contents that fill a container. AT: "they became very angry" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **and cried out** - "shouted out loud"
  * **The whole city was filled with confusion** - Here "city" refers to the people, and the people are spoken of as if they were a container. And, "confusion" is spoken of as if it were the contents that filled a container. AT: "Then people all over the city became upset and started shouting" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the people rushed together** - This was a mob or near riot situation.
  * **into the theater** - The Ephesus theater was used for public meetings and for entertainment such as plays and music. It was an outdoor semi-circular complex with bench seats that could accommodate thousands of people.
  * **They had seized Paul's travel companions** - "The people had seized the men who traveled with Paul"
  * **Gaius and Aristarchus** - These are names of men. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names|How to Translate Names]])
  * **who came from Macedonia** - Gaius and Aristarchus came from Macedonia but were working with Paul in Ephesus at this time.
