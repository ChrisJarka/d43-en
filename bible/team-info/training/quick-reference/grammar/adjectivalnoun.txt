====== Adjectival Noun ======


===== Description =====

An adjectival noun is a noun that acts like an adjective and describes another noun. A noun is a person, place or thing. An adjective is a word that describes a noun. A noun that describes another noun is acting like an adjective and so that is why it is called an “adjectival noun”.

===== Bible Examples =====

Field reference numbers are included for clarity purposes only. They are omitted in the actual note.

=== Rut:02:20 ===

"And Naomi said to her daughter-in-law, 'May he be blessed by Yahweh, who has not left off his loyalty to the living and to the dead.'"

  * **(1) to the living and the dead**  - **(3)** Naomi and Ruth were the "living" and Naomi's husband and sons were the "dead."  **(4)** These adjectival nouns could be translated as "to the people who are still alive and to the people who have already died."  **(5)** (See: Adjectival Noun)

=== Rut:01:08 ===

"May Yahweh show loyalty toward you, as you have shown loyalty toward the dead and toward me."

  * **(1) toward the dead and to me**  -  **(4)** This could be translated by replacing the adjectival noun "dead" with the verb "die": "to your husbands who died, and to me."  **(5)** (See: Adjectival Noun)
