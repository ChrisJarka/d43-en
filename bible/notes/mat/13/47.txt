===== Translation Notes: =====

Jesus went into a house with his disciples and continued to explain the parables about the kingdom of God.

  * In this parable, Jesus again uses a simile to teach his disciples what the kingdom of heaven is like. (See: Simile)
  * **like a net that was cast into the sea **- This can be translated with an active verb: "like some fishermen casting a net into the sea" (see UDB).
  * **net**  - This would have been a large net for catching fish, with floats on the top and weights on the bottom that was dragged through the water by more than one person.
  * **cast **- "thrown"
  * **sea **- "lake"
  * **that gathered **- "the net caught all kinds of fish"
  * **creatures of every kind**  - This can be translated "fish of every kind" or "all kinds of fish" (UDB). The word "creatures" (or "fish") is implied; it is not in the original Greek text. (See: Implicit and Explicit Information)
  * **When it was filled **- The pronoun "it" refers to the net (see ).
  * **drew up on the beach**  - "pulled the net up onto the beach" or "pulled the net ashore"
  * **the good things **- This can be translated "the good fish." The word "things" (or "kinds" or "fish") is implied; it is not in the original Greek text. (See: Implicit and Explicit Information)
  * **containers**  - "buckets" (UDB)
  * **the worthless things **- This can be translated "the worthless ones" (UDB) or "the worthless fish." The word "things" is implied; it is not in the original Greek text. (See: Implicit and Explicit Information)
  * **the worthless**  - This can be translated "the bad" or "the inedible."
  * **threw away**  - "cast out"
