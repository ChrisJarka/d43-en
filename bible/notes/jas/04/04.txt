===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **You adulteresses! -**  This is a metaphor that compares believers who do not obey God with a wife who sleeps with a man that is not her husband. This could be translated as "You are not being loyal to God!" (See: [[en:ta:translate:metaphor]])
  * ** Don't you know** - This is a rhetorical question that James uses to scold his audience. It can be translated as "You already know." (See: [[en:ta:translate:rhetoricalquestion]])
  * **friendship with the world is hostility against God** - Here "friendship" is a metonym and it refers to belonging to or acting like something else. Also here "world" is a metonym and refers to all the values and behaviors that do not honor God. This could be translated as "to act like those who dishonor God is to be someone who opposes God." (See: [[en:ta:translate:metonymy]]).
  * **to be a friend of the world** - "to act like those who dishonor God"
  * **Or do you think the scripture is useless** - This is a rhetorical question James uses to scold his audience. It can be translated as a statement: "Scripture is true."
  * **the human spirit God placed in us** - The reason God gave us this spirit can be made explicit: "the human spirit God placed in us so we could have a relationship with him." (See: [[en:ta:translate:implicitexplicit]])
