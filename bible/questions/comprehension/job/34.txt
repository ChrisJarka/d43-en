====== Job Chapter 34 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Who does Elihu want to listen to his words and hear him?

A. Elhu wants the wise men and those who have knowledge to listen to him. [34:1-3]

Q? What does Elihu want others to choose and discover for themselves?

A. Elihu wants them to choose what is just and discover what is good. [34:4]

Q? What does Job say that God has taken away from him even though he is without sin?

A. Job says that God has taken away his rights. [34:5-6]

Q? What are the things that Elihu is accusing Job of doing?

A. Elihu is accusing Job of drinking up mockery like water, going around in the company of those who do evil and walking with wicked men. [34:7-8]

Q? What does Elihu tell the men of understanding that God does not do?

A. Elihu tells them that God does nothing wicked and the Almighty does not pervert justice. [34:10-12]

Q? What does Elihu say would happen if God, who is in charge over the earth and the whole world, ever gathered back to himself his spirit and his breath?

A. Elihu says that if God gathered back to himself his spirit and his breath all flesh would perish and mankind would return to dust. [34:13-15]

Q? What questions about God does Elihu ask Job?

A. He asks them if one who hates justice can govern and if they can condemn God who is righteous and mighty. [34:16-17]

Q? Who does Elihu say are the works of God's hands?

A. Elihu says that leaders, rich and poor are all the work of God's hands. [34:19]

Q? What does Elihu say that God sees and where can the doers of iniquity hide themselves.

A. Elihu says that God sees a person's ways and there is no darkness or thick gloom where a doer of iniquite can hide. [34:21-22]

Q? Why does Elihu say about God's need to examine a person further before he goes before God in judgment?

A. Elihu says that there is no need for God to examine a person further and there is no need for any person to go before God in judjment. [34:23]

Q? What does Elihu say that God does to mighty men whose ways and deeds he knows?

A. God overthrows them in the night and they are destroyed. [34:24-25]

Q? What does he say that God will do to those who do wicked deeds like criminals and make the cry of the poor come to him?

A. Elihu says that God will kill them in the open sight of others. [34:26-28]

Q? Over whom does God rule and why does he not allow a godless man to rule according to Elihu?

A. According to Elihu, God rules over the nation and the individual alike and does not allow a godless man to rule so that the godless man may not entrap the people. [34:29-30]

Q? What is Elihu suggesting that Job should admit to God?

A. Elihu suggests that Job should admit that he is guilty and committed sin but would do it no longer. [34:31-32]
