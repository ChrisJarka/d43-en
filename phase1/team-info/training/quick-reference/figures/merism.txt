====== Merism ======


===== Description =====

Merism is a figure of speech in which a person refers to something by speaking of two extreme parts of it. By referring to the extreme parts, the speaker intends to include also everything in between those parts.

(Translation Academy: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])

=== Luke 24:16 ===

"But their eyes were prevented from recognizing him."

  * **(1) Their eyes were prevented from recognizing him**  - **(2)**  "Their eyes were kept from recognizing Jesus." **(3)**  The men's ability to recognize Jesus is spoken of as their eyes' ability to recognize him. **(4)** AT: "They were kept from recognizing him" or "Something prevented them so they could not recognize him." **(5)**  (See: Synecdoche)

=== Philippians 2:11 ===

And every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

  * **(1) every tongue**  - **(3)**  The word "tongue" is used to refer to the whole person. **(4)** AT: "every person" or "every being." **(5)**  (See: Synecdoche)
