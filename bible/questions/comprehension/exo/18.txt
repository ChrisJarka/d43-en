====== Exodus Chapter 18 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Who was Jethro?

A. Jethro was the priest of Midian, Moses’ father-in-law [18:1].

Q? Who sent Zipporah home?

A. Moses sent her home [18:2].

Q? Where did Jethro bring Moses' sons and his wife?

A. Jethro brought Moses' sons and his wife to Moses in the wilderness where he was camped at the mountain of God [18:6].

Q? How did Moses greet his father-in-law?

A. He bowed down, and kissed him [18:7].

Q? What did Jethro rejoice over?

A. Jethro rejoiced over all the good that Yahweh had done for Israel, in that he had rescued them from the Egyptians' power [18:9].

Q? How does Jethro know that Yahweh was greater than all the gods?

A. Jethro knows that Yahweh is greater than all the gods, because when the Egyptians treated the Israelites arrogantly, God rescued his people [18:11].

Q? Who ate the meal before God?

A. Aaron and all the elders of Israel came to eat a meal before God with Moses’ father-in-law [18:12].

Q? How long did the people stand around Moses as he sat down to judge the people?

A. The people stood around Moses from morning until evening  [18:13].

Q? When do people come to Moses?

A. The people come to Moses to ask for God's direction. When they have a dispute, they come to him [18:15-16].

Q? Why did Moses' father-in-law say that what he is doing is not good?

A. What Moses is doing is not very good because he will certainly wear out himself and the people who come to him, because the burden is too heavy for him. He cannot do it himself alone [18:17-18].

Q? What kind of men must Moses choose to put over the people?

A. Moses must choose capable men from all the people, men who honor God, men of truth who hate unjust gain [18:21].

Q? What cases will the capable men judge?

A. The capable men will judge the people in all routine cases, but the difficult cases they will bring to Moses. As for all the small cases, they can judge those themselves [18:22].
