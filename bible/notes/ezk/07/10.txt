===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Behold**  - This can be translated as “Look” or “Listen” or “Pay attention to what I am about to tell you.”
  * **rod**  - a figurative reference to the priesthood?
  * **The day is coming**  - The words "The day" are speaking of the time that God will punish Israel.  "The day of your punishment is coming" (See: [[en:bible:team-info:training:quick-reference:discourse:implicit-explicit]]) (See: [[en:bible:team-info:training:quick-reference:figures:metonymy]])
  * ** Doom has gone forth**  - The word "doom" refers to the certainty of Israel's destruction.  The words "has gone forth" refers to the fact that what causes their destruction has already begun to happen.  The phrase speaks of doom as something that is moving but doom is a concept and cannot move. "The cause of Israel's destruction has begun." (See: [[en:bible:team-info:training:quick-reference:figures:synecdoche]]) (See: [[en:bible:team-info:training:quick-reference:figures:personification]]) (See: [[en:ta:translate:implicitexplicit]])
  * **blossom of pride **- a figurative reference to the sin of pride in the priesthood?
  * **rod of wickedness**  - a figurative reference to corruption of the priesthood?
  * **The rod has bloomed...Violence has grown up**  - These phrases refer to Israel's wicked actions being to the point where God has judged them and is going to punish them.
