===== Translation Notes: =====

This begins a lengthy section comparing Assyria to a tree.

  * **Behold**  - This marks the beginning of another event in the larger story. It may involve different people than in previous events. Your language may have a way to of this.
  * **Assyria was a cedar in Lebanon <font 14px line-height: 19px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>with beautiful branches and a forested shade and great height!</font>    And its tree tops were between the branches**- "Assyria was powerful". This shows the power and mejesty of Assyria. (See:Metaphor)
  * **deep**  - the sea
  * **rivers went all around its planting areas** <font 14px line-height: 19px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>- This indicates that its fields had more than enough water</font>
  * **channel** <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>-</font>    <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>a deep part of a river, harbor, or other body of water where ships can go</font>
  * <font inherit/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>** stretched out to all the trees of the field  ** - Assyria was able to supply for the needs of other nations and this increased her strength.</font>
