===== translationNotes: =====

  * **For who knows whether he will be a wise man or a fool?** - The author uses this rhetorical question to emphasize that no one knows the character the man who will inherit his wealth. AT: "For no one knows whether he will be a wise man or a fool." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **he will be** - The word "he" refers to the author's heir. 
  * **under the sun** - This refers to things that are done on earth. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:ecc:01:01|1:3]]. AT: "on the earth" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **that my work and wisdom have built** - Here the author's "work" are "wisdom" represent himself and emphasize the things he did in his wisdom. AT: "that I worked very hard and wisely to build" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **vapor** - "mist." The authors speaks of things as being useless and meaningless as if they were 'vapor.' See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:ecc:01:12|1:14]]. AT: "as useless as vapor" or "meaningless" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **my heart began to despair** - Here the author refers to himself by his "heart" to emphasize his feelings. AT: "I began to despair" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
