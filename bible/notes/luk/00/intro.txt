====== Introduction to Luke ======

===== Part 1: General Introduction =====

=== Outline of the Book of Luke ===

  - Introduction and purpose for writing (1:1–4)
  - Birth of Jesus and his preparation for his ministry (1:5–4:13)
  - Galilean ministry (4:14–9:50)
  - Journey to Jerusalem
    - Discipleship (9:51–11:13)
    - Conflict and Jesus’ grief (11:14–14:35)
    - Parables and lost and found, honesty and dishonesty  (15:1–16:31)
    - Kingdom of God (17:1–19:27)
    - Entry to Jerusalem (19:28–44)
  - Jesus in Jerusalem (19:45–21:4)
  - Teaching about Second Coming (21:5–36)
  - Jesus’ death, burial, and resurrection (22:1–24:53)

=== What is the Book of Luke about? ===

The Book of Luke is one of four books in the New Testament that describe the life of Jesus Christ.  Each one of them is called a “gospel,” which means “good news” and tells about the life and the teaching of Jesus Christ from a different point of view. The Gospel of Luke the first of two volumes written by Luke, Acts being the second. Luke (as Acts) was addressed to “Theophilus” translated, “One who loves God,” and he may have been an anonymous benefactor who helped Luke write these important books. Luke selected certain teachings of Jesus and he focused on examples regarding forgiveness and prayer.

=== Who wrote the Book of Luke? ===

The author is Luke, who was a medical doctor. His way of writing and his excellent Greek shows that he was an educated man.  Luke wanted to write a accurate record of the life of Jesus, telling the events in the way they happened, so that others could benefit from reading it (1:1-3).  He was a “dear friend” of the Apostle Paul, (see Colossians 4:10-14). He was probably a Gentile who was sympathetic to Judaism, but he was not a convert to Judaism.  He explains many Jewish customs to the reader (see 1:8).  Though he was not an eyewitness to the ministry of Jesus, but as he wrote his accounts, he consulted many people who were.
