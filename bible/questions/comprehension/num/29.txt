====== Numbers Chapter 29 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Why did the people of Israel have a holy assembly on the first day of the seventh month, and what did they not do and what did they do on that day?

A. On the first day of the seventh month the people of Israel had a holy assembly to honor Yahweh, they did no normal work, they blew  trumpets. [29:1]

Q? On that day,what animals did they offer and why did they offer it?

A. On that day, they offered a burnt offering of one young bull, one ram, and seven male lambs a year old--each without blemish--to produce a sweet aroma for Yahweh. [29:2]

Q? What was the grain offering that they offered with the animals?

A. They offered a grain offering of fine four mixed with oil with the animals. [29:3]

Q? Why did they offer one male goat?

A. They offered one male goat as a sin offering to make atonement for themselves. [29:4]

Q? When were they to make this sin offering in addition to all of the usual offerings they were to make each month?

A. They were to make this sin offering in the seventh month in addition to all of the usual offerings they were to make each month. [29:6]

Q? What did Yahweh say the people of Israel had do on the tenth day of the seventh month?
 
A. On the tenth day of the seventh month the people of Israel had to have a holy assembly to honor Yahweh, humble themselves, and do no work. [29:7]

Q? What animals did they have to offer as a burnt offering to produce a sweet aroma for Yahweh on the fifteenth day of the seventh month?

A. On the fifteenth day of the seventh month the people of Israel had to offer offer one young bull, one ram, and seven male lambs a year old without any blemish. [29:8] 

Q? What did the people of Israel have to put with the animals?

A. The people of Israel had to put flour mixed with oil with each of the animals.  [29:9-10]

Q? Why was the male goat given? 

A. The male goat was given as a sin offering. [29:11]

Q? On what day was there another holy assembly to honor Yahweh in which they did no normal work and this time kept the festival for him for seven days.

A. On the fifteenth day of the seventh month there was another  holy assembly to honor Yahweh in which they did no normal work and kept the festival for him seven days. [29:12] 

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the second day of the assembly?

A. On the second day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer twelve young bulls, two rams, and fourteen male lambs without blemish, a grain offering and the drink offerings as commanded, a male goat as a sin offering in addition to the regular burnt offering, its grain offering and their drink offering. [29:17-19]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the third day of the assembly?

A. On the third day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer eleven bulls, and all the sacrifices of the previous day. [29:20-22]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the fourth day of the assembly?

A. On the fourth day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer ten bulls and all the secrifices of the previous day. [29:23-25]

Q? How should the people of the Israel celebrate the fifth day of the assembly?

A. On the fifth day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer nine bulls and all the sacrifices of the previous day. [29:26-28]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the sixth day of the assembly?

A. On the sixth day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer eight bulls and all the sacrifices of the previous day. [29:29-31]

Q? How should the people of Israel celebrate the seventh day of the assembly?

A. On the seventh day of the assembly the people of Israel must offer seven bulls and all the sacrifices of the previous day. [29:32-34]

Q? How should the the people of Israel celebrate the eighth day of the assembly?

A. On the eighth day of the assembly the people of Israel must have another solemn assembly, do no normal work, make a burnt offering of one bull, one ram and seven male lambs without blemish, their grain offering and drink offerings as commanded, one male goat as a sin offering in addition to the regular burnt offering, its grain offering, and their drink offerings. [29:35-38]

Q? What must the people of Israel offer to Yahweh at their fixed festivals?

A. The people of Israel must offer their burnt offerings, grain offerings, drink offerings, and fellowship offerings in addition to their vows and free will offerings at their fixed festivals. [29:39-40]
