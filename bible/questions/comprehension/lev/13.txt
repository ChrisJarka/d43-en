====== Leviticus Chapter 13 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What did Yahweh say must be done if one has on his skin a swelling, scab or bright spot that becomes infected?

A. Yahweh said that anyone who has a skin infection must come to one of the priests who must examine the place on the skin to determine if it is infectious, and if so, will declare the person unclean. [13:2,3]

Q? What if the priest determines that the swelling, scab or bright spot may not be infectious, what must be done?

A. If the priest cannot determine that the swelling, scab, or bright spot is infectious, the person must be isolated for one week to be examined again. [13:4]

Q? What if the swelling, scab or bright spot proves not to be infectious?

A. If the swelling, scab, or bright spot is not infectious, the priest will pronounce him clean after he washes his clothes. [13:6]

Q? What are the three conditions that the swelling, scab, or bright spot may show if it is to be declared infectious?

A. The three conditions which the swelling, scab, or bright spot may show to be declared infectious are white swelling in the skin, the hair has turned white, and there is raw fleash in te swelling. [13:9,10]

Q? If the priest determines that this is a chronic skin disease, what must the priest do?

A. If the priest determines that this is a chronic skin disease, he must declare the person unclean, but does not isolate him. [13:11]

Q? If the skin disease covers the person's entire body and raw flesh can be seen,  is the person clean?

A. If the skin disease covers the person's entire body and raw flesh can be seen, the person is unclean. [13:14]
