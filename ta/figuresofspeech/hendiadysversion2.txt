====== Hendiadys [henˈdīədəs] ======
In hendiadys a single idea is expressed with two words connected with “and,” when one word could be used to modify the other.

===== Purposes of Hendiadys =====


=====Examples from English=====
**Nice and soft**\\ 
means "nicely soft."

**Sat and talked**\\ 
means "sat talking."


===== Examples from the Bible =====

**for I will give you a mouth and wisdom which all your adversaries will not be able to withstand or to contradict.** Luke 21:15a (WEB)\\ 
Jesus said that he would give them “a mouth and wisdom”. This is an example of hendiadys. The word “mouth” here refers to “words,” or "what to say," and the phrase could be translated as “wise words” or "wise things to say".


**to the end that you should walk worthily of God, who calls you into his own kingdom and glory.** 1 Thes 2:12 (WEB)\\ 
Paul wrote about God calling us into his own "kingdom and glory”. God's kingdom and his glory are not two separate things that God calls us into. Rather his kingdom is glorious. The phrase "kingdom and glory" is an example of hendiadys. Some versions translate this as "glorious kingdom".
