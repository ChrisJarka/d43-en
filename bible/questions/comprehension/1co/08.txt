====== 1 Corinthians Chapter 08 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What subject is addressed in chapter 8?

A. The subject of eating food sacrificed to idols is addressed in chapter eight [8:1 & 4]

Q? What do knowledge and love do?

A. Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up [8:1].

Q? Is an idol in this world a God?

A. An idol in this world is nothing and there is no God but one [8:4].

Q? For the Christian who is the one God?

A. For us there is only one God the Father. From him are all things, and we live for him [8:6].

Q? For Christians who is the one Lord?

A. For the Christian there is one Lord Jesus Christ, through whom all things exist, and through whom we exist [8:6].

Q? What happens when some who practiced idol worship eat certain food as if it had been something sacrificed to an idol?

A. Their conscience is thereby corrupted because it is weak [8:7].

Q? Does the food we eat make us better or worse to God?

A. Food will not recommend us to God. We are not worse if we do not eat, nor better if we do eat it [8:8].

Q? What should we be careful of concerning our freedom?

A. We should be careful that our freedom does not become a reason for someone who is weak in faith to stumble [8:9].

Q? What happens when a brother or sister with a weak conscience concerning meat sacrificed to idols, eats that meat because they see you eating it?

A. You are causing that brother or sister with a weak conscience to be destroyed [8:10-11].

Q? Who do you sin against when you knowingly cause a brother or sister in Christ to stumble because of their weak conscience?

A. You are sinning against the brother or sister you caused to stumble and you are sinning against Christ [8:11-12].

Q? What does Paul say he will do if food causes his brother or sister to stumble?

A. Pauls says if his food causes his brother or sister to stumble, he will never eat meat again [8:13].
