===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Behold**  -This word adds emphasis to what follows. It can also be translated as "indeed."
  * **your face**  - This phrase can be used to mean "you."  (See: Synecdoche)
  * **I have made your face as stubborn as their faces and your brow as hard as their brows **- In this sentence, "you" and "your" refer to Ezekiel.  "Their" refers to the people of Israel.  Alternate translation: "I will make you as unyeilding and and hardened as the people of Israel."
  * **brow **  - forehead
  * **I have made your brow like a diamond, harder than flint**  - "I have made your brow like the hardest stone, harder than flint." (See: Simile)
  * **flint **- a hard stone used for sharpening knives.
  * **Do not fear them â€¦ or their faces **- The words "their" and "their faces" refer to the people of Israel.
