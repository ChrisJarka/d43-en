====== Acts 24 General Notes ======


=== Structure and formatting ===

This chapter functions as a type of legal defense that Paul give concerning his actions. He lays out the fact refuting the charges brought against him.

=== Special concepts in this chapter ===

== Respect ==
The Jewish leaders showed great respect to the Gentile leaders and show no respect to Paul, a Jewish and Christian leader. 

=== Other possible translation difficulties in this chapter ===

== Governmental leaders ==
This chapter and this narrative storyline has many different government officials with different levels of power or influence over the charges brought against Paul. This will make translating the various titles difficult to correspond to the translators's cultural background.

== Implicit knowledge ==
The statement, "Go away for the present, but when I have time again, I will call you" implies that the governor is too busy to make a decision about the case against Paul. The reader should understand that this is a weak excuse. The author goes on to explain that he hoped Paul was wealth and could bribe his way out of prison. It took the governor two years to decide on the outcome of the charges against Paul.
