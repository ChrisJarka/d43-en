====== The Sinless Sacrifice [49-11] ======

{{:en:obs:obs-49-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

**[[:en:obe:kt:jesus|Jesus]]** never **[[:en:obe:kt:sin|sinned]]**, but he chose to be **[[:en:obe:other:punish|punished]]** and to **[[:en:obe:kt:death|die]]** as the perfect **[[:en:obe:other:sacrifice|sacrifice]]** to **take away** your sins and the sins of every person in the world. Because Jesus sacrificed himself, **[[:en:obe:kt:god|God]]** can **[[:en:obe:kt:forgive|forgive]]** any sin, even terrible sins.


===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **take away** - That is, "remove the penalty of" or, "remove the punishment for." The sacrifice of Jesus causes God to look at our sin as if it never existed.
