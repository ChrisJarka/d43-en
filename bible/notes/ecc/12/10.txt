===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **The words of the wise...taught by one shepherd** - The writer compares the teacher who uses his words to instruct people to a shepherd who uses his tools to lead his flock. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **The words of the wise are like goads. Like nails driven deeply are the words of the masters in collections of their proverbs** - These two sentences mean basically the same thing and are combined for emphasis. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **The words of the wise are like goads** - The things that a wise person says can be painful but they push people to make the right decisions, just like people use a stick with a nail on the end to prod an animal. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **Like nails driven deeply** - This phrase describes the nails that stick out of the ends of goads. AT: "Like nails that a person has driven deeply" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **the words of the masters in collections of their proverbs** - "the words of those who are experts on collections of proverbs"
  * **which are taught by one shepherd** - AT: "which one shepherd teaches" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
